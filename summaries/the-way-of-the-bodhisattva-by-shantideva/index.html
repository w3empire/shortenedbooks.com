The Way of the Bodhisattva by Shantideva (Summary & Review)

author David Barrett
Rating

“The Way of the Bodhisattva” is a profound Mahayana Buddhist text written by the Indian scholar-monk Shantideva in the 8th century. It serves as a guide for those aspiring to attain enlightenment and embody the qualities of a bodhisattva, an enlightened being who selflessly works for the liberation and well-being of all sentient beings.

The book consists of ten chapters, each exploring different aspects of the bodhisattva path and providing teachings and practices to cultivate compassion, wisdom, and ethical conduct.

Shantideva’s intention in writing this book was to inspire and guide individuals on the path of awakening, encouraging them to develop the altruistic aspiration to benefit all beings and to transcend their own self-centeredness.

The book is grounded in the understanding that genuine happiness and freedom can only be attained through the realization of interconnectedness and the cultivation of compassion towards all beings.

Idea 1: Cultivating Bodhicitta

The first major idea presented in the book is the cultivation of bodhicitta, or the awakened mind. Shantideva emphasizes that the ultimate purpose of engaging in spiritual practice is to attain enlightenment not only for oneself but also for the sake of others.

Bodhicitta is the altruistic intention to achieve this awakening in order to alleviate the suffering of all sentient beings. Shantideva explores the methods to generate and nurture bodhicitta, highlighting its essential role in the bodhisattva path.

Bodhicitta serves as the foundation for all other practices, as it acts as a powerful motivation to engage in virtuous actions and overcome self-centeredness. Shantideva emphasizes that the path of the bodhisattva is rooted in compassion and that the cultivation of bodhicitta is indispensable in transforming one’s own mind and benefiting others.

Idea 2: Perfection of Patience

Another significant idea presented in the book is the perfection of patience. Shantideva explains that patience is a crucial virtue for a bodhisattva, as it enables them to endure hardships, insults, and other unfavorable circumstances without anger or resentment.

The cultivation of patience is seen as an antidote to the afflictions of anger and hostility, allowing individuals to respond to difficulties with equanimity and compassion.

Shantideva encourages the practice of patience by highlighting its transformative power in personal growth and the positive impact it has on others. He presents various contemplations and reflections to develop patience, such as contemplating the impermanent nature of circumstances and the interconnectedness of all beings.

By cultivating patience, individuals can overcome reactivity, foster inner peace, and create harmonious relationships with others.

Idea 3: Emptiness and Ultimate Reality

Shantideva delves into the profound concept of emptiness, which is a fundamental teaching in Mahayana Buddhism. He elucidates the ultimate reality of all phenomena, emphasizing that they lack inherent existence and are dependently originated.

Understanding emptiness allows one to transcend the illusion of a fixed and independent self, leading to the realization of interconnectedness and interdependence.

Shantideva emphasizes that realizing emptiness is crucial for the development of wisdom and the dismantling of ignorance. By investigating the nature of reality, individuals can let go of attachment, aversion, and other afflictive emotions that arise from a distorted perception of the world.

The contemplation of emptiness serves as a transformative practice that helps individuals cultivate insight and compassion, leading to the alleviation of suffering for themselves and others.

Idea 4: Cultivating Ethical Conduct

Ethical conduct holds a central place in the bodhisattva path, and Shantideva highlights its significance as another essential idea in the book. He outlines the importance of moral discipline and virtuous behavior, emphasizing that ethical conduct forms the basis for a compassionate and awakened life.

The cultivation of ethical conduct involves refraining from harmful actions, engaging in beneficial actions, and upholding precepts that promote harmony, honesty, and non-harm.

Shantideva explains that ethical conduct contributes to inner purification and the cultivation of a calm and focused mind. By upholding virtuous behavior, individuals create conducive conditions for their own well-being and the well-being of others. Ethical conduct is seen as an expression of compassion and a means to foster a supportive and harmonious environment in which the bodhisattva path can thrive.

Idea 5: Joyful Perseverance

Joyful perseverance, or enthusiastic effort, is another key idea presented by Shantideva in the book. He emphasizes the importance of exerting diligent effort in spiritual practice, overcoming laziness, and maintaining enthusiasm in the face of challenges. Joyful perseverance allows individuals to sustain their commitment to the path, even when faced with obstacles or discouragement.

Shantideva encourages individuals to cultivate a sense of joy and inspiration in their practice, recognizing that the bodhisattva path is a path of great purpose and profound meaning. By nurturing enthusiasm, individuals can overcome doubt and mental obstacles, allowing them to progress steadily on the path of awakening. Joyful perseverance brings energy and vitality to one’s spiritual journey, fostering inner strength and resilience.

Idea 6: Cultivating Equanimity

Equanimity is explored as an important quality for a bodhisattva to develop. Shantideva teaches that equanimity involves seeing all beings as equal in their desire for happiness and freedom from suffering, irrespective of their social status, race, or other superficial differences. Cultivating equanimity helps overcome biased attitudes and partiality, fostering an inclusive and unbiased approach to others.

Shantideva highlights the importance of letting go of attachment and aversion in order to cultivate genuine equanimity. By recognizing the inherent dignity and worth of all beings, individuals can cultivate a sense of impartiality and treat others with fairness and compassion.

Equanimity provides a foundation for developing genuine love and compassion towards all beings by breaking down the barriers that separate us and fostering a sense of interconnectedness.

Idea 7: Cultivating Wisdom

Wisdom is regarded as a central pillar of the bodhisattva path, and Shantideva dedicates a significant portion of the book to its exploration. He elucidates the nature of wisdom and the methods to cultivate it, emphasizing that wisdom arises through a deep understanding of impermanence, suffering, and the interdependent nature of all phenomena.

Shantideva teaches that wisdom involves transcending the limitations of conceptual thinking and perceiving the world directly as it is. Through insight and contemplative practices, individuals can penetrate the illusion of a fixed and independent self and develop a profound understanding of the nature of reality.

Wisdom serves as a transformative force that liberates individuals from ignorance and delusion, allowing them to live in alignment with truth and compassion.

Idea 8: Compassion and Bodhisattva Vows

Compassion is at the heart of the bodhisattva path, and Shantideva emphasizes its crucial role in transforming oneself and benefiting others. He explores the nature of compassion and the various methods to cultivate it, encouraging individuals to develop genuine empathy and concern for the suffering of all beings.

Shantideva introduces the concept of bodhisattva vows, which are commitments to embody the qualities and engage in the actions of a bodhisattva. These vows serve as a guideline for ethical conduct, compassion, and the cultivation of wisdom. By taking these vows, individuals make a solemn commitment to work tirelessly for the benefit of all beings and to refrain from actions that harm themselves and others.

Idea 9: Engaging Skillful Means

Skillful means, or upaya, are an essential idea explored in the book. Shantideva discusses the importance of adapting one’s actions and teachings according to the needs and capacities of others. Skillful means involve employing various methods to guide individuals towards awakening, taking into account their unique dispositions and circumstances.

Shantideva emphasizes the importance of cultivating skillful means to communicate effectively, teach the Dharma, and alleviate the suffering of others. Skillful means involve employing compassion, wisdom, and discernment to skillfully respond to the diverse needs and challenges encountered on the bodhisattva path.

Idea 10: Transcending Dualistic Thinking

The final idea from the book is the transcendence of dualistic thinking. Shantideva emphasizes the need to go beyond the limitations of dualistic concepts such as good and bad, self and others, and gain and loss. By transcending dualistic thinking, individuals can develop a more expansive and inclusive perspective that recognizes the interconnectedness of all phenomena.

Shantideva highlights the importance of cultivating non-discriminatory awareness, where one sees the inherent interconnectedness and interdependence of all beings and phenomena. This transcendent perspective helps to break down the barriers that separate us from others and fosters a deep sense of compassion and unity.

Who is this Book Recommended For?

“The Way of the Bodhisattva” is recommended for anyone interested in deepening their understanding of Mahayana Buddhism and exploring the path of awakening. It is particularly valuable for individuals who aspire to cultivate compassion, wisdom, and ethical conduct in their lives and engage in actions that benefit others.

This book is beneficial for spiritual practitioners of all levels, from beginners to advanced practitioners. It provides comprehensive teachings and practical guidance on the bodhisattva path, offering inspiration and insights for those seeking to live a more compassionate and meaningful life.

The book can be a valuable resource for those engaged in Buddhist studies, meditation, and the exploration of the altruistic ideals of the bodhisattva.

Review of the Book

“The Way of the Bodhisattva” is a profound and insightful guide to the bodhisattva path. Shantideva’s teachings are presented in a clear and accessible manner, making the book accessible to both seasoned practitioners and those new to Buddhism.

The text is rich with wisdom and practical guidance, offering a comprehensive exploration of the qualities, practices, and attitudes necessary for the realization of the bodhisattva ideal.

I like how this book places a lot of emphasis on the cultivation of compassion and the recognition of interconnectedness. Shantideva’s teachings on bodhicitta and compassion are particularly inspiring, reminding readers of the transformative power of love and kindness.

The practical instructions and meditative practices provided throughout the book serve as valuable tools for individuals seeking to integrate these qualities into their lives.

But it is worth noting that “The Way of the Bodhisattva” can be a challenging read for some, as it delves into profound philosophical concepts and requires contemplation and reflection. The text can be dense at times, and readers may benefit from additional commentaries or teachings to deepen their understanding.

Also, the book’s language and cultural context are rooted in eighth-century India, which may require some effort to bridge the historical and cultural gap for contemporary readers.

To improve the book’s engagement with diverse readers, it could be beneficial to include more examples and stories that resonate with a wide range of cultural backgrounds. This would help to make the teachings more relatable and accessible to readers from different walks of life.

Plus, providing practical exercises and reflections at the end of each chapter could facilitate the application of the teachings in everyday life.

Conclusion

“The Way of the Bodhisattva” by Shantideva is a timeless and invaluable guide for those aspiring to walk the path of awakening. Its teachings on compassion, wisdom, and ethical conduct offer a profound roadmap for individuals seeking to live a life of meaning, purpose, and service to others. The book invites readers to cultivate the qualities of a bodhisattva and embark on a journey of transformation and selfless action.

With its emphasis on love, compassion, and the realization of interconnectedness, “The Way of the Bodhisattva” holds significant relevance in contemporary society. In a world that often feels divided and disconnected, the teachings of Shantideva remind us of the potential for transformation and the power of our actions to create a more compassionate and harmonious world.

By embracing the principles and practices presented in this book, individuals can contribute to their own well-being and the well-being of others, cultivating a more compassionate and awakened society. “The Way of the Bodhisattva” serves as a profound and timeless guide, illuminating the path towards liberation and the awakening of the bodhisattva within each of us.
