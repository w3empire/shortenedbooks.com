Organize Tomorrow Today by Jason Selk (Summary & Review)

author Mark Wheeler
Rating

“Organize Tomorrow Today: 8 Ways to Retrain Your Mind to Optimize Performance at Work and in Life” is a self-help book written by Jason Selk, Tom Bartow, and Matthew Rudy. The book aims to provide readers with practical strategies and techniques to enhance their performance both professionally and personally.

The authors, who have extensive experience in sports psychology and performance coaching, offer valuable insights on how to develop mental toughness, increase productivity, and achieve long-term success.

The book revolves around the central idea that one’s mindset plays a crucial role in achieving goals and maintaining peak performance. It emphasizes the importance of developing a proactive mindset, setting clear objectives, and implementing effective strategies to overcome obstacles and distractions.

By following the principles outlined in the book, readers can learn to optimize their mental and physical resources, enhance focus and concentration, and ultimately improve their overall performance in various areas of life.

Idea 1: The Importance of a Proactive Mindset

The first idea presented in “Organize Tomorrow Today” centers around the significance of cultivating a proactive mindset. The authors argue that individuals who take a proactive approach to their work and personal lives are more likely to achieve success and overcome challenges.

They emphasize the need to focus on controllable factors, such as attitude, effort, and preparation, rather than being reactive to external circumstances.

To develop a proactive mindset, the authors suggest adopting a structured approach to goal setting. They emphasize the importance of setting specific, measurable, attainable, relevant, and time-bound (SMART) goals.

By clearly defining objectives and creating action plans, individuals can increase their motivation, maintain focus, and track their progress effectively.

Practical exercise: Take time to reflect on your current mindset and identify areas where you tend to be reactive rather than proactive. Set aside a specific time each day to plan and prioritize your tasks, focusing on the most important and impactful actions. Monitor your progress and adjust your approach as necessary.

Idea 2: Creating an Ideal Performance State

The second idea explored in the book revolves around creating an ideal performance state. The authors argue that individuals perform at their best when they are in an optimal mental and physical state. They highlight the importance of managing energy levels, maintaining focus, and minimizing distractions to achieve peak performance consistently.

The authors introduce the concept of the “Relaxation Response,” a state of calm and focused awareness that allows individuals to perform at their best. They provide strategies for managing stress and suggest incorporating relaxation techniques, such as deep breathing exercises and visualization, into daily routines.

By practicing these techniques, individuals can reduce anxiety, enhance concentration, and improve their overall performance.

Practical exercise: Dedicate a few minutes each day to practicing deep breathing exercises or mindfulness meditation. Create a calming ritual before engaging in important tasks, such as listening to soothing music or visualizing a successful outcome. Monitor how these practices impact your focus and performance over time.

Idea 3: Implementing a Weekly Mental Workout

The third idea presented in the book focuses on implementing a weekly mental workout to enhance performance. The authors argue that just as physical exercise is essential for maintaining physical fitness, regular mental exercise is crucial for strengthening mental resilience and cognitive abilities.

The authors propose a specific mental workout routine called the “Three Rs: Reminder, Routine, and Reward.”

  • The “Reminder” involves identifying a specific time and place to engage in mental exercises.
  • The “Routine” consists of performing a series of mental exercises, such as positive self-talk, visualization, and goal review.
  • The “Reward” provides a positive reinforcement mechanism to motivate and reinforce the mental workout routine.

Practical exercise: Designate a specific day and time each week for your mental workout routine. Create a checklist of mental exercises you would like to incorporate, such as positive affirmations, visualization of success, and reviewing your goals. Track your adherence to the routine and notice the impact on your mental resilience and performance.

Idea 4: Developing Focus and Eliminating Distractions

The fourth idea explored in the book centers around developing focus and eliminating distractions. The authors highlight the importance of maintaining a concentrated and distraction-free mindset to maximize productivity and performance.

They provide practical strategies for managing external distractions, such as turning off notifications, establishing dedicated workspaces, and managing interruptions effectively.

The authors also address internal distractions, including negative self-talk and self-doubt.

They introduce the concept of “Thought Stopping,” a technique aimed at recognizing and interrupting negative thoughts to replace them with positive and empowering ones. By implementing these strategies, individuals can improve their focus, increase productivity, and achieve better results in their professional and personal endeavors.

Practical exercise: Identify the most common external and internal distractions that hinder your focus and productivity. Take steps to eliminate or minimize these distractions by setting boundaries, creating a conducive work environment, and implementing strategies to manage negative self-talk. Monitor your ability to maintain focus and measure the impact on your performance.

Idea 5: Establishing a Sleep Routine for Optimal Performance

The fifth idea presented in the book emphasizes the significance of establishing a sleep routine for optimal performance. The authors argue that adequate sleep is essential for cognitive functioning, memory consolidation, and overall well-being. They provide insights into the importance of sleep hygiene, the impact of sleep deprivation on performance, and strategies for improving sleep quality.

The authors recommend setting consistent sleep and wake times, creating a relaxing bedtime routine, and optimizing sleep environment conditions to promote restful sleep. They also discuss the benefits of power naps and the importance of scheduling breaks throughout the day to enhance productivity and mental clarity.

Practical exercise: Evaluate your current sleep routine and identify areas for improvement. Establish consistent sleep and wake times that allow for sufficient rest. Create a bedtime routine that includes relaxation activities, such as reading or listening to soothing music. Implement strategies to optimize your sleep environment, such as minimizing noise and maintaining a comfortable temperature.

Idea 6: The Power of Visualization and Self-Talk

The sixth idea explored in the book focuses on the power of visualization and self-talk in optimizing performance. The authors highlight the impact of mental imagery and positive self-talk on confidence, motivation, and goal attainment. They provide practical techniques for incorporating visualization and self-talk into daily routines to enhance performance.

The authors suggest creating vivid mental images of success, envisioning the process of achieving goals, and imagining overcoming obstacles. They also emphasize the importance of positive self-talk and provide strategies for replacing negative thoughts with empowering affirmations.

By consistently practicing visualization and self-talk, individuals can boost their confidence, increase motivation, and improve performance outcomes.

Practical exercise: Take a few minutes each day to engage in visualization exercises. Close your eyes and imagine yourself successfully accomplishing your goals, focusing on the details and emotions associated with the achievement. Incorporate positive affirmations into your self-talk, replacing self-limiting thoughts with empowering statements. Observe the impact of these practices on your confidence and performance over time.

Idea 7: Harnessing the Power of Routines and Habits

The seventh idea presented in the book centers around harnessing the power of routines and habits to optimize performance. The authors argue that well-designed routines and habits can significantly contribute to productivity, efficiency, and goal attainment. They emphasize the importance of creating intentional habits and structuring routines that align with one’s objectives.

The authors provide guidance on habit formation, habit stacking, and the creation of a daily routine that maximizes productivity and minimizes decision fatigue. They also discuss the role of discipline and consistency in developing and maintaining effective habits. By implementing intentional routines and habits, individuals can streamline their actions, reduce cognitive load, and optimize their performance.

Practical exercise: Identify key areas of your life where you would like to develop intentional habits. Start with small, achievable habits and gradually build on them. Create a daily routine that incorporates these habits, ensuring they align with your goals and priorities. Monitor the impact of these routines and habits on your productivity and overall performance.

Idea 8: Embracing Resilience and Overcoming Setbacks

The eighth idea explored in the book focuses on embracing resilience and overcoming setbacks. The authors emphasize that setbacks and failures are inevitable in the pursuit of success, and developing resilience is essential for bouncing back and maintaining performance momentum. They provide strategies for reframing setbacks, managing emotions, and developing a growth mindset.

The authors suggest practicing reframing techniques to view setbacks as opportunities for growth and learning. They also emphasize the importance of emotional management, including stress reduction strategies and seeking support when needed. By cultivating resilience and adopting a growth mindset, individuals can navigate challenges effectively, learn from failures, and maintain long-term performance success.

Practical exercise: Reflect on a recent setback or failure and apply reframing techniques to view it as an opportunity for growth. Practice stress reduction techniques, such as deep breathing or engaging in physical activity, to manage emotions during challenging times. Seek support from mentors, peers, or professionals to gain perspective and guidance. Notice how these strategies impact your resilience and ability to overcome setbacks.

Who is this Book Recommended For?

“Organize Tomorrow Today” is recommended for individuals who strive for improved performance and productivity in their professional and personal lives. The book is particularly valuable for:

  • Professionals seeking strategies to enhance focus, overcome distractions, and achieve optimal performance at work.
  • Entrepreneurs and business owners aiming to improve time management, goal setting, and overall productivity.
  • Athletes and sports enthusiasts looking to develop mental toughness, enhance performance, and manage pressure effectively.
  • Students and educators interested in techniques to boost concentration, memory, and learning outcomes.
  • Anyone interested in personal development and self-improvement who seeks practical strategies to optimize their performance in various areas of life.

Review of the Book

“Organize Tomorrow Today” offers valuable insights and practical strategies for optimizing performance. The book combines principles from sports psychology, performance coaching, and personal development to provide a comprehensive framework for achieving success. The authors present the information in a clear and accessible manner, making it easy for readers to understand and apply the concepts.

One aspect that works well in the book is the inclusion of practical exercises and techniques throughout each chapter. These exercises allow readers to actively engage with the material and apply the strategies to their own lives. The authors provide step-by-step guidance, making it easier for readers to implement the ideas presented.

However, one potential drawback of the book is the heavy emphasis on sports analogies and examples. While this approach may resonate with athletes and sports enthusiasts, it may be less relatable to readers from other backgrounds. Additionally, some readers may find the structure of the book repetitive, as the eight main ideas follow a similar format.

“Organize Tomorrow Today” is a valuable resource for individuals seeking to optimize their performance and achieve their goals. The book offers practical strategies, supported by research and real-life examples, to enhance productivity, focus, and mental resilience. By following the principles outlined in the book, readers can retrain their minds and develop the habits necessary for long-term success.

Conclusion

“Organize Tomorrow Today: 8 Ways to Retrain Your Mind to Optimize Performance at Work and in Life” provides a comprehensive guide for individuals looking to enhance their performance and achieve their goals.

The book emphasizes the importance of mindset, goal setting, routines, and mental resilience in optimizing performance. Through practical exercises and techniques, readers can develop proactive mindsets, manage distractions, improve focus, and overcome setbacks.

The book is recommended for a wide range of individuals, including professionals, entrepreneurs, athletes, students, and anyone interested in personal development. While the heavy reliance on sports analogies may limit its appeal to some readers, the practicality and clarity of the strategies presented make it a valuable resource for those seeking self-improvement.

To put it simply, “Organize Tomorrow Today” provides actionable advice and a roadmap for readers to retrain their minds, optimize their performance, and achieve long-term success in various areas of life.

By implementing the eight main ideas outlined in the book, individuals can transform their mindset, habits, and productivity, ultimately leading to enhanced performance and fulfillment.
