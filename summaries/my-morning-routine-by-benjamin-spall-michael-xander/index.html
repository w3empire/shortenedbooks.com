My Morning Routine by Benjamin Spall (Summary & Review)

author David Barrett
Rating

“My Morning Routine: How Successful People Start Every Day Inspired” is a book co-authored by Benjamin Spall and Michael Xander. The book explores the morning routines of various successful individuals from different walks of life, including entrepreneurs, artists, CEOs, and athletes.

The authors aim to uncover the habits, rituals, and mindsets that these successful people employ to kick-start their day and set themselves up for productivity, creativity, and overall well-being.

Through interviews and in-depth research, Spall and Xander present a collection of morning routines that offer valuable insights into how successful individuals structure their mornings to optimize their physical and mental health, enhance their focus, and cultivate a positive mindset.

The book emphasizes that a well-designed morning routine can have a profound impact on one’s productivity, energy levels, and overall quality of life.

The authors explore a range of topics related to morning routines, including the benefits of waking up early, the importance of mindfulness and meditation, the power of exercise and movement, the significance of nourishing the body with a healthy breakfast, and the role of technology in our morning rituals.

By showcasing a diverse array of routines, the book encourages readers to experiment with different strategies and tailor their own morning routines to align with their unique goals and preferences.

Idea 1: The Power of Waking Up Early

One recurring theme in “My Morning Routine” is the power of waking up early. Many of the successful individuals featured in the book attribute a significant part of their achievements to rising early and utilizing the extra hours in the morning to engage in activities that set a positive tone for the day.

Waking up early allows for quiet solitude and uninterrupted time for self-reflection, planning, and personal growth. By starting the day ahead of the rush and distractions, individuals can set themselves up for a productive and focused day.

Example: Tim Cook, the CEO of Apple, wakes up at 4:30 a.m. every day to get a head start on his day. He believes that waking up early provides him with uninterrupted time for exercise and reading, which helps him maintain mental clarity and make better decisions throughout the day.

Practical exercise: Set a goal to wake up 30 minutes earlier than your usual time for a week and use that time for activities that bring you joy, such as reading, writing, or engaging in a hobby. Notice how this extra time affects your mood and productivity throughout the day.

Idea 2: Mindfulness and Meditation

Another key aspect of successful morning routines is the practice of mindfulness and meditation. Many individuals find value in starting their day with a period of calm and introspection to cultivate a clear and focused mind. Mindfulness and meditation techniques help reduce stress, enhance mental clarity, and promote a sense of presence and well-being.

Example: Arianna Huffington, co-founder of The Huffington Post, practices mindfulness meditation for at least 10 minutes every morning. This practice allows her to set her intentions for the day, reduce anxiety, and approach her work with a sense of groundedness and perspective.

Practical exercise: Start your morning with a short meditation or mindfulness practice. Find a quiet space, sit comfortably, and focus your attention on your breath or a specific sensation in your body. Allow thoughts to come and go without judgment, bringing your attention back to the present moment whenever your mind wanders.

Idea 3: Exercise and Movement

Physical exercise and movement play a vital role in many successful morning routines. Engaging in physical activity early in the day boosts energy levels, improves mood, and enhances cognitive function. Exercise also promotes better sleep patterns and overall physical well-being.

Example: Olympic gold medalist Usain Bolt starts his day with a morning workout routine that includes stretching, running, and weightlifting. He believes that physical exercise sets a positive tone for the day, boosts his confidence, and helps him stay disciplined in other areas of his life.

Practical exercise: Incorporate at least 10 minutes of movement into your morning routine. It could be a brisk walk, a short yoga session, or a quick bodyweight workout. Notice how this physical activity impacts your energy levels and mental focus throughout the day.

Idea 4: Nourishing Breakfast

A nourishing breakfast is a common thread among successful morning routines. The food we consume in the morning provides essential nutrients to fuel our bodies and minds for the day ahead. A balanced breakfast helps stabilize blood sugar levels, improves concentration, and supports overall well-being.

Example: American television host and author Oprah Winfrey starts her day with a nutritious breakfast that typically includes fruits, whole grains, and a source of protein. She understands the importance of nourishing her body with wholesome foods to maintain energy and mental clarity.

Practical exercise: Plan and prepare a healthy breakfast the night before, ensuring it includes a combination of complex carbohydrates, protein, and healthy fats. Enjoy this nourishing meal in the morning, and observe how it impacts your energy and focus throughout the day.

Idea 5: Digital Detox

Many successful individuals emphasize the importance of reducing digital distractions in the morning to create a space for clarity and focus. Detaching from technology allows individuals to be more present, reduces information overload, and sets boundaries between personal and professional lives.

Example: Jack Dorsey, the co-founder of Twitter and Square, has a strict morning routine that includes no technology for the first few hours of his day. By disconnecting from his phone and email, he prioritizes his well-being and creative thinking.

Practical exercise: Establish a “no-phone” or “no-email” policy for the first hour of your day. Instead, engage in activities that nourish your mind and body, such as reading a book, journaling, or enjoying a quiet cup of tea. Notice how this intentional break from technology affects your overall well-being and focus.

Idea 6: Prioritizing Important Tasks

Successful individuals often structure their morning routines to tackle their most important tasks or creative work first. By prioritizing these tasks, they can make progress on their goals and avoid getting consumed by reactive and less important activities later in the day.

Example: Writer and entrepreneur Seth Godin dedicates his mornings to writing, focusing on his most important creative work. He recognizes that his cognitive energy and focus are highest in the morning, allowing him to produce his best work during those hours.

Practical exercise: Identify one important task or goal you have been putting off and commit to working on it for at least 30 minutes every morning for a week. Observe how starting your day with this focused work impacts your productivity and sense of accomplishment.

Idea 7: Creating Rituals and Consistency

Morning routines often involve specific rituals and habits that provide a sense of structure and consistency. These rituals signal the brain and body that it is time to transition from rest to active engagement, setting the tone for a productive and intentional day.

Example: World-renowned chef and restaurateur Thomas Keller starts his mornings by brewing a fresh cup of coffee and enjoying it in silence. This ritual helps him create a sense of calm and reflection before diving into the demands of his day.

Practical exercise: Identify one simple ritual you can incorporate into your morning routine, such as lighting a candle, practicing gratitude, or taking a few deep breaths. Repeat this ritual every morning for a week and observe how it enhances your overall sense of well-being and intentionality.

Idea 8: Journaling and Reflection

Journaling and reflection serve as valuable tools for self-awareness, goal-setting, and tracking progress. Many successful individuals allocate time in their morning routines for introspection, gratitude practice, and capturing their thoughts and ideas on paper.

Example: Bestselling author and entrepreneur Tim Ferriss starts his day with a journaling practice he calls “Morning Pages.” He writes down his thoughts, fears, and goals to gain clarity and direction for the day ahead.

Practical exercise: Dedicate 10 minutes each morning to journaling. Write freely about your thoughts, emotions, and aspirations. Additionally, express gratitude by jotting down three things you are grateful for each day. Notice the impact this practice has on your overall mindset and self-awareness.

Idea 9: Mindful Transitions

Successful individuals recognize the importance of mindful transitions between different activities and responsibilities throughout the day. They use morning routines to intentionally transition from rest to work, setting a positive and focused tone for the hours ahead.

Example: Entrepreneur and author Marie Forleo incorporates a transition ritual into her morning routine. Before diving into work, she takes a few moments to stretch, do a quick dance, or engage in any activity that helps her shift from relaxation to action.

Practical exercise: Introduce a transition ritual into your morning routine. It could be a short walk, listening to an energizing song, or performing a few stretches. Notice how this intentional transition impacts your focus and productivity during the day.

Idea 10: Customizing Your Morning Routine

Ultimately, the key to a successful morning routine lies in customization. While there are common themes and strategies, each individual’s routine should be tailored to their unique needs, preferences, and goals. Experimentation and self-reflection are crucial in designing a morning routine that works best for you.

Example: Professional tennis player Serena Williams structures her morning routine around her body’s natural rhythm. She listens to her body and adapts her routine based on factors like travel schedules and tournament commitments.

Practical exercise: Reflect on your current morning routine and consider what elements work well for you and what could be improved. Experiment with different strategies and activities to customize your routine to align with your specific goals and aspirations.

Who is this Book Recommended For?

“My Morning Routine” is recommended for individuals seeking to optimize their mornings and create a positive foundation for their day. It is particularly valuable for those who are interested in learning from the habits and rituals of successful individuals across various fields.

Whether you are an entrepreneur, an artist, a student, or a professional, this book offers practical insights and inspiration to design a morning routine that supports your well-being, productivity, and personal growth.

Review of the Book

“My Morning Routine” provides a comprehensive and inspiring exploration of how successful individuals approach their mornings. The book offers a diverse range of perspectives and routines, making it relatable and applicable to a wide audience.

The authors’ commitment to interviewing and researching a variety of individuals ensures that readers gain insights from different industries, backgrounds, and lifestyles.

One thing that is awesome about this book is the practical nature of its content. The authors not only showcase morning routines but also distill the underlying principles and strategies that readers can apply to their own lives.

The book’s compelling examples and useful tips enable readers to put the concepts into practice and customize them to their particular situation.

However, the book’s focus on successful individuals may make it seem unattainable for some readers. While it is inspiring to learn from accomplished individuals, it is important to remember that morning routines should be personalized and flexible.

The book could have placed more emphasis on the underlying principles and science behind morning routines to make it accessible to a wider range of readers.

Conclusion

“My Morning Routine: How Successful People Start Every Day Inspired” is a valuable resource for anyone seeking to create a meaningful and productive morning routine. It offers practical insights, inspiring examples, and actionable strategies that readers can adapt to their own lives.

By exploring the habits and rituals of successful individuals, the book highlights the power of intentional mornings in shaping one’s overall well-being, productivity, and success.

Whether you are a student, an entrepreneur, or a busy professional, this book provides guidance and inspiration to design a morning routine that sets you up for a fulfilling and successful day.
