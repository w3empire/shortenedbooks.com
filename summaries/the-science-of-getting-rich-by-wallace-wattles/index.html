The Science of Getting Rich Summary of Key Ideas and Review - Wallace Wattles

author Jared Wright
Rating

“The Science of Getting Rich” is a self-help book written by Wallace Wattles and first published in 1910. The book aims to provide readers with a practical and philosophical guide to achieving financial abundance and success.

Unlike many other books on wealth creation, Wattles focuses not only on the material aspects but also on the mental and spiritual principles that contribute to financial prosperity.

Wattles emphasizes that wealth creation is not a matter of chance or luck but rather a science that can be understood and applied by anyone. The author argues that by aligning one’s thoughts, beliefs, and actions with the principles of abundance, individuals can attract wealth and create a life of prosperity.

Throughout the book, Wattles combines metaphysical concepts with practical advice, making it accessible to a wide range of readers.

Idea 1: The Power of Thought and Visualization

According to Wattles, the starting point for wealth creation is the power of thought. He asserts that all things in the universe are first created in the mind before they manifest in reality. Therefore, to attract wealth, individuals must cultivate a mindset of abundance.

This involves focusing on positive thoughts and visualizing oneself already in possession of the desired wealth. By consistently holding these mental images, individuals can harness the creative power of their thoughts and bring their desires into physical existence.

Idea 2: The Principle of Gratitude

Gratitude is a central theme in “The Science of Getting Rich.” Wattles argues that expressing gratitude for what one already has is essential for attracting more abundance. By appreciating and acknowledging the blessings in one’s life, individuals send out positive vibrations that resonate with the universe and draw in greater wealth.

Gratitude shifts the focus from lack to abundance and creates a mindset of receptivity, allowing individuals to receive more of what they desire.

Idea 3: Taking Purposeful Action

While the book emphasizes the importance of thoughts and gratitude, it also emphasizes the necessity of taking action. Wattles asserts that merely visualizing wealth without corresponding action is insufficient.

To create lasting prosperity, individuals must align their actions with their desires and work towards their goals. However, the author emphasizes that action should be taken with a sense of purpose and in harmony with the natural flow of life. By acting in accordance with one’s vision, individuals can bring their dreams into reality.

Idea 4: The Right to Be Rich

Wattles challenges the notion that wealth is limited or immoral. He argues that everyone has the right to be rich and that poverty is not virtuous. According to the author, embracing a mindset of abundance and actively pursuing wealth not only benefits individuals but also contributes to the advancement of society as a whole.

Wattles emphasizes that acquiring riches does not mean taking from others but rather creating value and contributing to their well-being.

Idea 5: Overcoming the Competitive Mindset

One of the thought-provoking ideas in the book is Wattles’ perspective on competition. He suggests that individuals should move away from a competitive mindset and instead focus on creating and providing value.

Rather than trying to outdo others or acquire wealth at their expense, individuals should seek to collaborate and offer unique contributions to the world. Wattles argues that by focusing on service and adding value, individuals can attract wealth naturally and build lasting success.

Idea 6: The Law of Attraction

Central to “The Science of Getting Rich” is the concept of the Law of Attraction. Wattles posits that like attracts like and that individuals attract into their lives whatever they consistently think about and focus upon.

By aligning one’s thoughts, beliefs, and actions with the desired outcome, individuals can harness the Law of Attraction and manifest their aspirations. This idea aligns with the principles of the New Thought movement, which gained popularity during the late 19th and early 20th centuries.

Idea 7: Abundance and the Natural World

Wattles draws upon nature to illustrate his principles of abundance. He emphasizes that the universe is inherently abundant, with countless resources available to all. Just as nature continually replenishes itself, individuals can tap into this infinite abundance by aligning their thoughts and actions with the natural laws of prosperity.

By observing the cycles of nature and understanding its abundance, individuals can cultivate a deep sense of faith and trust in the limitless possibilities for wealth creation.

Idea 8: The Importance of Acting in the Present Moment

Wattles stresses the significance of living in the present moment. He advises against dwelling on past failures or worrying about future outcomes, as it diverts energy and focus from the present opportunities.

The author encourages individuals to seize the present moment and take inspired action towards their goals. By fully engaging with the present, individuals can make the most of their creative power and increase their chances of achieving success.

Idea 9: Financial Success as a Measure of Personal Development

In “The Science of Getting Rich,” Wattles suggests that financial success is not merely a material achievement but also an indicator of personal growth and development. He asserts that as individuals strive to acquire wealth, they must also develop their character, talents, and abilities. According to Wattles, the process of wealth creation requires individuals to become more capable, efficient, and deserving.

He views financial success as a reflection of one’s growth as a person and encourages readers to embrace the journey of self-improvement.

Idea 10: Living a Fulfilling and Abundant Life

Ultimately, Wattles’ teachings in “The Science of Getting Rich” go beyond monetary wealth. He emphasizes that true prosperity encompasses all aspects of life, including health, relationships, and personal fulfillment.

The book encourages readers to seek holistic abundance and to live a life of purpose, joy, and gratitude. According to Wattles, true success lies in the ability to align one’s thoughts and actions with the principles of abundance, leading to a harmonious and prosperous existence.

Who is this Book Recommended For?

“The Science of Getting Rich” is recommended for individuals seeking to improve their financial situation and manifest greater abundance in their lives. It is particularly valuable for those who are open to exploring metaphysical concepts and willing to combine them with practical strategies. This book is suitable for readers of all backgrounds, as it presents its ideas in a straightforward and accessible manner.

Additionally, individuals who are interested in personal development, self-improvement, and the Law of Attraction will find value in this book. It offers a unique perspective on wealth creation that goes beyond traditional financial advice, emphasizing the importance of mindset, gratitude, and purposeful action. Readers who are willing to challenge conventional beliefs about money and embrace a more expansive view of wealth will resonate with the ideas presented in this book.

Review of the Book

“The Science of Getting Rich” presents a compelling and thought-provoking approach to wealth creation. The book’s strengths lie in its emphasis on the power of thoughts, visualization, and gratitude. Wattles provides practical techniques and exercises that readers can implement to shift their mindset and attract abundance.

The book’s timeless principles continue to resonate with individuals who are seeking to transform their financial circumstances.

But just so you know, some readers may find the book’s metaphysical concepts challenging to accept or apply. The ideas presented in “The Science of Getting Rich” require a degree of openness to alternative perspectives and a willingness to explore beyond conventional wisdom.

Some people may also find the language and examples used by the author to be dated, as the book was written over a century ago. However, the underlying principles remain relevant and can be adapted to contemporary life.

Despite these potential limitations, “The Science of Getting Rich” offers valuable insights into the relationship between mindset, action, and financial abundance. It encourages readers to take responsibility for their own wealth creation and challenges societal beliefs about wealth and poverty.

The book’s strength lies in its holistic approach, which addresses not only financial success but also personal growth, fulfillment, and the pursuit of a purpose-driven life.

Conclusion

“The Science of Getting Rich” by Wallace Wattles is a timeless guide to wealth creation that combines metaphysical principles with practical strategies. Through its emphasis on mindset, gratitude, and purposeful action, the book provides readers with a roadmap to attract abundance and create a life of prosperity.

While some readers may find its concepts challenging, those who are open to exploring alternative perspectives and willing to take responsibility for their financial success will find value in this book.

Ultimately, “The Science of Getting Rich” reminds us that true wealth encompasses all aspects of life and invites readers to align their thoughts, beliefs, and actions with the principles of abundance.
