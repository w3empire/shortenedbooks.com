Fully Engaged by Thomas Sterner (Summary & Review)

author Dean Stewart
Rating

“Fully Engaged: Using the Practicing Mind in Daily Life” is a self-help book written by Thomas Sterner. The book explores the concept of mindfulness and its practical application in various aspects of life.

Sterner emphasizes the importance of fully engaging in the present moment and adopting a mindset of continuous improvement through deliberate practice. The author aims to guide readers in developing a focused and disciplined approach to their daily activities, leading to increased productivity, satisfaction, and a deeper sense of fulfillment.

Sterner introduces the concept of the “practicing mind,” which involves staying present and fully engaged in the process rather than fixating on the end result. By shifting the focus from outcome to effort, individuals can cultivate a mindset of growth and progress. The book emphasizes the idea that mastery and excellence are achieved through consistent practice and embracing the challenges that arise along the way.

Throughout the book, Sterner explores various areas of life where the practicing mind can be applied, including work, relationships, creativity, and personal development. He provides practical strategies and exercises to help readers develop mindfulness, discipline, and patience.

By incorporating these principles into their daily routines, readers can learn to appreciate the journey, find joy in the process, and achieve long-term success and fulfillment.

Idea 1: Embracing the Process

Embracing the process is a key idea in “Fully Engaged.” Sterner highlights the importance of shifting one’s focus from the end goal to the present moment and the steps involved in achieving it. Instead of being fixated on the outcome, you need to fully engage in the process and appreciate each step along the way.

The concept of embracing the process can be applied to various areas of life. For example, if you’re working on a project, rather than obsessing over the final result, focus on the tasks at hand and take pleasure in the progress you’re making. By doing so, you develop a greater sense of satisfaction and enjoyment in the process itself, rather than relying solely on the outcome for fulfillment.

Example: Imagine you’re learning to play a musical instrument. Instead of getting discouraged by your mistakes or comparing yourself to accomplished musicians, embrace the process of practice. Focus on the small improvements you make each day and find joy in the act of playing, rather than fixating on reaching a certain level of proficiency.

Practical exercise: Choose an activity or project you’re currently engaged in and consciously shift your focus to the process. Take a few moments each day to reflect on the small steps you’ve taken and appreciate the effort you’re putting in. Notice how this shift in mindset affects your motivation and satisfaction.

Idea 2: Cultivating Patience

Cultivating patience is another crucial aspect discussed in the book. Sterner emphasizes the need to develop patience when facing challenges or pursuing long-term goals. He suggests that impatience often leads to frustration, hasty decision-making, and a lack of enjoyment in the present moment.

Patience involves accepting that progress takes time and being willing to persevere through setbacks and obstacles. By cultivating patience, you develop resilience and a greater capacity to handle difficulties. This mindset shift allows you to maintain focus and continue making progress, even when faced with setbacks or slow progress.

Example: Let’s say you’re working on a fitness goal, such as running a marathon. Instead of expecting immediate results or getting frustrated when progress seems slow, cultivate patience. Understand that building endurance and improving your fitness level is a gradual process. Focus on consistent training, celebrate small milestones, and trust in the progress you’re making over time.

Practical exercise: Choose a goal or project that requires long-term commitment. Write down the steps you need to take to achieve it and create a timeline. Remind yourself regularly to cultivate patience and maintain focus on the process rather than rushing to achieve the end result.

Idea 3: Overcoming Resistance and Distractions

In this idea, Sterner addresses the challenges of overcoming resistance and distractions that hinder progress. He highlights the importance of discipline and self-awareness in combating these obstacles.

Resistance often arises when we face tasks that are difficult or outside our comfort zone. It manifests as procrastination, self-doubt, or seeking instant gratification. Sterner advises acknowledging the resistance and committing to take small, consistent steps toward your goals, even when it feels uncomfortable.

Distractions, both external and internal, can also derail our focus and productivity. Sterner suggests creating an environment that minimizes distractions and establishing rituals or routines that help you stay engaged and present with the task at hand.

Example: Let’s say you’re working on a writing project and you find yourself frequently distracted by social media notifications. Instead of succumbing to the distractions, create a dedicated workspace free from distractions, turn off notifications, and establish specific periods of focused work. By consciously removing distractions and creating a conducive environment, you increase your ability to stay fully engaged.

Practical exercise: Identify a task or goal that you tend to resist or find distracting. Break it down into smaller, manageable steps. Commit to spending a specific amount of focused time each day on that task without succumbing to distractions. Gradually increase the duration of your focused work sessions as you build discipline and overcome resistance.

Idea 4: Applying Mindfulness to Relationships

The author emphasizes the application of mindfulness to relationships. He discusses the importance of being fully present and attentive when interacting with others, whether it’s with family, friends, or colleagues.

Mindful communication involves actively listening to others, suspending judgment, and giving them your full attention. By doing so, you create deeper connections and foster understanding and empathy in your relationships. Sterner encourages readers to let go of distractions and preconceived notions, focus on the person in front of them, and genuinely engage in the conversation.

Example: Imagine you’re having a conversation with a loved one who is sharing their thoughts and feelings with you. Instead of thinking about your response or being preoccupied with your own concerns, practice being fully present. Listen attentively, maintain eye contact, and show genuine interest in what they are saying. By doing so, you strengthen your bond and deepen the quality of your connection.

Practical exercise: Choose a relationship in your life that you want to improve or strengthen. Practice mindful communication by being fully present and attentive during your interactions. Make a conscious effort to listen actively, ask questions, and respond with empathy and understanding. Notice how this approach enhances the quality of your relationship.

Idea 5: Finding Joy in the Journey

Finding joy in the journey is a central theme in “Fully Engaged.” Sterner encourages readers to shift their focus from external achievements to the intrinsic joy of the process itself. He argues that true fulfillment comes from embracing the challenges, growth, and learning that occur along the way.

When you find joy in the journey, you develop a sustainable source of motivation and satisfaction. Sterner suggests appreciating the small victories, celebrating progress, and acknowledging the effort you’re putting in. By cultivating gratitude for the present moment, you enhance your overall well-being and enjoyment of life.

Example: Consider a project or goal you’re currently pursuing. Rather than solely fixating on the desired outcome, focus on the joy of the process. Embrace the opportunities for growth and learning that arise. Celebrate milestones, even the small ones, and express gratitude for the journey itself. By finding joy in the process, you develop a more sustainable and fulfilling approach to achieving your goals.

Practical exercise: Choose a project or goal that you’re currently working on. Write down three things you appreciate about the process or journey towards that goal. Each day, reflect on those aspects and express gratitude for the opportunities they bring. Notice how this shift in perspective affects your overall enjoyment and motivation.

Idea 6: Developing a Growth Mindset

A growth mindset is characterized by the belief that abilities and intelligence can be developed through dedication and effort. Sterner suggests that adopting a growth mindset allows you to embrace challenges, learn from failures, and continuously improve.

A fixed mindset, on the other hand, assumes that abilities are fixed and unchangeable. This mindset often leads to fear of failure and avoidance of challenges. By cultivating a growth mindset, you open yourself up to new possibilities and see setbacks as opportunities for growth rather than personal shortcomings.

Example: Imagine you’re learning a new skill, such as painting. Instead of believing that you either have artistic talent or you don’t, adopt a growth mindset. Embrace the challenges that come with learning and view mistakes as valuable learning experiences. By doing so, you create a positive and empowering environment for personal growth and development.

Practical exercise: Identify an area of your life where you tend to have a fixed mindset. Challenge those limiting beliefs by reframing them with a growth mindset. For example, if you believe you’re not good at public speaking, reframe it as an opportunity to develop your communication skills and practice. Take small steps towards improvement and celebrate the progress you make along the way.

Idea 7: Applying Deliberate Practice

Sterner introduces the concept of deliberate practice, a methodical approach to improving skills and abilities. Deliberate practice involves breaking down tasks into specific components, focusing on those components individually, and consistently pushing beyond your comfort zone.

The author suggests that deliberate practice is more effective than mindless repetition. By identifying specific areas for improvement and actively engaging in deliberate practice, you can accelerate your learning and skill development. Sterner encourages readers to set clear goals, seek feedback, and maintain a disciplined practice routine.

Example: Let’s say you’re learning a new language. Instead of mindlessly repeating vocabulary words, engage in deliberate practice. Break down the language learning process into components like pronunciation, grammar, and conversational skills. Focus on one component at a time, seek resources for targeted practice, and challenge yourself with increasingly complex tasks. By adopting this deliberate approach, you enhance your language learning progress.

Practical exercise: Choose a skill or area of expertise you want to improve. Break it down into specific components and set goals for each component. Create a practice routine that involves targeted practice sessions, seeking feedback, and pushing beyond your comfort zone. Engage in deliberate practice consistently, and track your progress over time.

Idea 8: Maintaining Focus and Eliminating Multitasking

Maintaining focus and eliminating multitasking are essential principles discussed in “Fully Engaged.” Sterner highlights the detrimental effects of multitasking on productivity and effectiveness. He emphasizes the importance of directing your attention fully to the task at hand, avoiding distractions, and staying present in the moment.

When you eliminate multitasking and maintain focus, you improve the quality of your work and enhance your ability to solve problems and make decisions. Sterner suggests creating a conducive environment, establishing specific periods of uninterrupted work, and utilizing techniques such as time blocking to maximize focus and productivity.

Example: Suppose you’re working on a project that requires deep concentration. Instead of attempting to juggle multiple tasks simultaneously, create dedicated periods of focused work. Eliminate distractions, such as turning off notifications, closing unnecessary tabs on your computer, and finding a quiet workspace. By giving your full attention to one task at a time, you optimize your focus and productivity.

Practical exercise: Choose a task that requires focused attention and set a timer for 25 minutes. Commit to working on that task without any distractions or interruptions for the entire duration. Afterward, take a short break and repeat the process. Notice how this focused approach enhances your productivity and quality of work.

Idea 9: Embracing Imperfections and Learning from Failure

Sterner highlights the importance of embracing imperfections and learning from failure as essential components of growth and progress. He encourages readers to let go of perfectionism and the fear of making mistakes, recognizing that failures are valuable opportunities for learning and improvement.

By embracing imperfections, you alleviate self-imposed pressure and create a more compassionate and forgiving environment for yourself. Sterner suggests reframing failures as stepping stones on the path to success, allowing you to persevere and bounce back from setbacks.

Example: Let’s say you’re working on a creative project, and your initial attempt doesn’t meet your expectations. Instead of becoming discouraged or giving up, embrace your imperfections. View the first attempt as a valuable learning experience that provides insights for improvement. Adjust your approach and use the lessons learned to enhance subsequent iterations. By doing so, you foster resilience and growth in your creative endeavors.

Practical exercise: Choose a task or project where you tend to be overly critical of yourself. Set an intention to embrace imperfections and view failures as opportunities for growth. When faced with setbacks or mistakes, ask yourself what you can learn from them and how you can use those lessons to improve. Notice how this shift in perspective enhances your resilience and motivation.

Idea 10: Applying Mindfulness to Daily Activities

The final idea discussed in “Fully Engaged” is the application of mindfulness to daily activities. Sterner emphasizes that mindfulness is not limited to meditation but can be integrated into every aspect of life. By bringing awareness and intentionality to even the simplest tasks, you cultivate a sense of presence and engagement.

Mindful daily activities involve paying attention to the sensations, thoughts, and emotions that arise in each moment. By doing so, you enhance your overall well-being, reduce stress, and develop a deeper appreciation for the present moment.

Example: Consider a routine activity you engage in daily, such as washing dishes or taking a shower. Rather than performing the task on autopilot, practice mindfulness. Notice the temperature of the water, the sensation of the soap on your hands, and the sound of running water. Engage your senses fully and bring your attention back to the present whenever your mind wanders. By incorporating mindfulness into daily activities, you create moments of presence and tranquility in your day.

Practical exercise: Choose a routine activity that you often perform mindlessly. Before engaging in the activity, take a moment to set the intention to be fully present and attentive. Throughout the activity, bring your attention back to the sensations, thoughts, and emotions that arise. Notice how this practice enhances your overall sense of presence and enjoyment.

Who is this Book Recommended For?

“Fully Engaged: Using the Practicing Mind in Daily Life” is recommended for individuals seeking practical guidance on developing mindfulness, discipline, and focus in their daily lives.

The book is valuable for those who wish to enhance their productivity, satisfaction, and overall well-being by adopting a mindset of continuous improvement. It is suitable for readers of all backgrounds and professions who are open to self-reflection and personal growth.

This book is particularly beneficial for individuals who struggle with perfectionism, impatience, and distractions. It offers valuable insights and strategies to overcome these challenges and cultivate a more mindful and engaged approach to various aspects of life, including work, relationships, and personal development.

Review of the Book

“Fully Engaged: Using the Practicing Mind in Daily Life” provides valuable insights and practical strategies for cultivating mindfulness and focus in everyday activities. The book’s emphasis on embracing the process, developing patience, and finding joy in the journey resonates deeply with readers seeking fulfillment and growth.

What we liked:

  • Clear and relatable examples that illustrate the concepts.
  • Practical exercises that provide actionable steps for implementation.
  • Emphasis on embracing the process and finding joy in the journey.

What needs improvement:

  • The organizational structure could be more logical and streamlined.
  • More in-depth scientific research or citations to support the strategies presented.

Conclusion

“Fully Engaged: Using the Practicing Mind in Daily Life” is an excellent read for individuals seeking to cultivate mindfulness, discipline, and focus in their daily activities. Through the exploration of concepts such as embracing the process, developing patience, and finding joy in the journey, the book provides practical guidance for personal growth and enhanced well-being.

Thomas Sterner’s emphasis on the practicing mind and the power of deliberate practice offers a fresh perspective on productivity and success. By shifting the focus from outcome to effort, readers can develop a mindset that values progress and continuous improvement.

While the book could benefit from improved organization and additional scientific research, its relatable examples and practical exercises make it accessible and applicable to readers from all walks of life. “Fully Engaged” serves as a good guide for those seeking to live more mindfully, find fulfillment in the present moment, and unlock their true potential.
