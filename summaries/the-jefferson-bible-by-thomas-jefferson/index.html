The Jefferson Bible by Thomas Jefferson (Summary & Review)

author David Barrett

“The Jefferson Bible: The Life and Morals of Jesus of Nazareth” is a book that was created by Thomas Jefferson, the third president of the United States. Jefferson was a Founding Father and an important figure in the American Revolution, known for his contributions to the drafting of the Declaration of Independence.

Jefferson was a deist, a religious belief that emphasizes the existence of a supreme being who does not intervene in the affairs of the world. He was also a critic of organized religion and believed that many of the miracles and supernatural elements in the Bible were the result of human error and interpretation.

In order to create his version of the Bible, Jefferson carefully cut out passages from the New Testament that he believed were authentic accounts of the life and teachings of Jesus of Nazareth. He eliminated all references to supernatural elements, such as miracles and the Resurrection, as well as passages that he believed were added later by the authors of the Gospels.

The resulting work, which Jefferson called “The Life and Morals of Jesus of Nazareth,” or simply “The Jefferson Bible,” was a slim volume that focused solely on the moral teachings of Jesus as a human being. It included selections from the Gospels of Matthew, Mark, Luke, and John, arranged in a chronological narrative of Jesus’ life.

“The Jefferson Bible” was not published during Jefferson’s lifetime, but it was eventually released in 1895, long after his death. It has since become a popular work, especially among those who are interested in Jefferson’s philosophy and his views on religion.

Idea 1: Jesus was a moral teacher whose teachings were based on love, compassion, and forgiveness.

Idea 2: The essence of Jesus’ teachings was the Golden Rule: “Do unto others as you would have them do unto you.”

Idea 3: Jesus emphasized the importance of humility and selflessness, and he taught that true greatness comes from serving others.

Idea 4: Jesus rejected the idea of a vengeful, wrathful God and instead taught that God is loving and merciful.

Idea 5: Jesus believed in the importance of living a simple and humble life, free from material possessions.

Idea 6: Jesus taught that the kingdom of God is not a physical place, but rather a state of being that can be achieved through inner transformation.

Idea 7: Jesus believed that everyone, regardless of their social status or background, was worthy of love and respect.

Idea 8: Jesus taught that true worship is not about rituals or ceremonies, but rather about living a life of love and service to others.

Idea 9: Jesus emphasized the importance of forgiveness, even in the face of great wrongs and injustices.

Idea 10: Jesus believed that the highest form of love is to lay down one’s life for others.
