Turning Pro by Steven Pressfield (Summary & Review)

author Mark Wheeler
Rating

“Turning Pro: Tap Your Inner Power and Create Your Life’s Work” is a self-help book written by Steven Pressfield. Building upon his previous work, “The War of Art,” Pressfield explores the concept of turning pro and the transformative power it holds for individuals striving to pursue their creative endeavors. The book serves as a guide to overcoming resistance, self-sabotage, and the various obstacles that hinder personal growth and artistic development.

Pressfield’s primary intention is to inspire readers to transition from an amateur mindset to a professional one in any chosen field. He believes that turning pro is not limited to artists alone but applies to all areas of life, including business, sports, and personal growth. The book presents a roadmap for embracing discipline, dedication, and the willingness to confront and overcome the internal and external challenges that stand in the way of achieving one’s potential.

Idea 1: Embracing Professionalism

The first idea explored in “Turning Pro” centers around embracing professionalism as a mindset and way of life. Pressfield emphasizes the importance of committing fully to one’s craft and approaching it with a serious, dedicated attitude. He encourages individuals to shed the amateur mindset and adopt the discipline and work ethic of professionals.

To illustrate this idea, Pressfield shares stories of renowned artists, athletes, and entrepreneurs who achieved greatness by embodying professionalism. He highlights the significance of consistency, perseverance, and the willingness to put in the necessary hours of practice and hard work.

Idea 2: Overcoming Resistance

One of the central themes in the book is the concept of resistance—the force that opposes our creative endeavors and personal growth. Pressfield delves deep into the various forms resistance takes, such as procrastination, self-doubt, fear, and distractions. He argues that resistance is an inherent part of the creative process and must be acknowledged and overcome to unlock one’s true potential.

Pressfield provides practical strategies and insights on how to recognize and combat resistance. He emphasizes the importance of developing a daily practice, establishing routines, and cultivating discipline as means to overcome resistance and maintain focus on the work at hand.

Idea 3: Finding One’s Calling

“Turning Pro” explores the idea of finding one’s calling—a unique and authentic path that aligns with an individual’s passions and purpose. Pressfield encourages readers to explore their deepest desires and confront the fears and doubts that may be holding them back from pursuing their true calling.

The book presents stories of individuals who discovered their calling and transformed their lives by embracing it. Pressfield believes that by tapping into our inner intuition and heeding the call of our passions, we can unlock our full potential and create a life that is deeply fulfilling and meaningful.

Idea 4: The Importance of Self-Validation

In this idea, Pressfield emphasizes the significance of self-validation and the need to detach from external validation. He argues that seeking approval and validation from others can be detrimental to the creative process and personal growth. Instead, he encourages individuals to focus on their inner compass and trust their instincts.

Pressfield suggests that by relying on self-validation and staying true to our own creative vision, we can overcome the fear of judgment and criticism. He urges readers to embrace vulnerability and authenticity in their work, understanding that the opinions of others should not define or hinder our creative journey.

Idea 5: Cultivating a Warrior Mindset

Pressfield explores the concept of cultivating a warrior mindset as an essential aspect of turning pro. He draws parallels between the mindset of a professional and that of a warrior, highlighting qualities such as resilience, determination, and the ability to face adversity head-on.

By adopting a warrior mindset, individuals can navigate the challenges, setbacks, and failures that come with pursuing their life’s work. Pressfield emphasizes the importance of developing mental toughness, embracing discomfort, and embracing the willingness to take risks and learn from failures.

Idea 6: Embracing the Shadow

“Turning Pro” delves into the concept of the shadow—the hidden aspects of ourselves that we may try to ignore or suppress. Pressfield argues that to truly turn pro and create our life’s work, we must confront and integrate our shadow aspects.

By acknowledging and accepting the darker parts of ourselves, we can tap into a deeper well of creativity and self-understanding. Pressfield encourages readers to explore their fears, insecurities, and past traumas, using them as catalysts for growth and transformation.

Idea 7: The Importance of Mentorship

Pressfield stresses the importance of seeking mentorship and learning from those who have already achieved mastery in our chosen fields. He emphasizes the value of learning from others’ experiences, mistakes, and wisdom.

The book highlights the power of finding mentors and guides who can provide valuable insights, feedback, and encouragement. Pressfield encourages readers to seek out mentors and engage in a lifelong process of learning and growth.

Idea 8: The Professional’s Code

In this idea, Pressfield presents the professional’s code—a set of principles and values that guide the behavior and mindset of a professional. He outlines qualities such as discipline, patience, humility, and the willingness to embrace failure and learn from it.

Pressfield argues that by adopting the professional’s code, individuals can elevate their work and create a foundation for long-term success and fulfillment. He emphasizes the importance of integrity, self-mastery, and the relentless pursuit of excellence.

Idea 9: Turning Pro in Everyday Life

“Turning Pro” extends beyond the realm of creative pursuits and explores how turning pro can impact all aspects of life. Pressfield encourages readers to approach their everyday lives with the same dedication, discipline, and professionalism they bring to their creative endeavors.

He argues that by turning pro in everyday life—whether it be relationships, health, or personal growth—we can achieve a sense of wholeness and alignment. Pressfield provides practical strategies and insights on how to apply the principles of turning pro to create a well-rounded and fulfilling life.

Idea 10: The Journey of Mastery

The final idea explored in “Turning Pro” centers around the journey of mastery. Pressfield emphasizes that turning pro is not a destination but an ongoing process of growth and evolution. He highlights the importance of embracing the journey, enjoying the process, and staying committed to continuous learning and improvement.

Pressfield encourages readers to view challenges, setbacks, and failures as opportunities for growth and refinement. He argues that by embracing the journey of mastery, individuals can create a life’s work that is ever-evolving and deeply fulfilling.

Bonus Section: 10 Exercises and Insights for Embracing Your Inner Pro and Creating Your Life’s Work

Here is a list of exercises, techniques, and insights based on the book “Turning Pro: Tap Your Inner Power and Create Your Life’s Work” by Steven Pressfield:

  1. Identify your Resistance: Take some time to reflect on the areas in your life where you consistently face resistance. It could be a creative project, a personal goal, or a habit you’ve been meaning to develop. Write down the specific forms of resistance that arise and the excuses or distractions that tend to derail your progress.
  2. Establish a Daily Practice: Commit to a daily practice that aligns with your goals and aspirations. It could be writing for a set amount of time, practicing a musical instrument, or engaging in physical exercise. Start with a small, manageable time commitment and gradually increase it over time. This consistent practice will help you build discipline and overcome resistance.
  3. Confront your Shadow: Explore the aspects of yourself that you tend to avoid or deny—the fears, insecurities, or past traumas that hold you back. Engage in self-reflection and journaling to gain a deeper understanding of these shadow aspects. Seek professional support if needed to navigate this process effectively.
  4. Find a Mentor: Identify individuals who have achieved mastery in your field of interest and reach out to them for guidance. Seek mentorship and learn from their experiences and insights. Forming relationships with mentors can provide invaluable support, accountability, and encouragement along your journey.
  5. Create a Professional’s Code: Develop a set of principles and values that guide your behavior and mindset as a professional. Define what professionalism means to you and outline the qualities you wish to embody, such as discipline, perseverance, and integrity. Regularly review and refine your professional code to stay aligned with your goals.
  6. Embrace Failure and Learn from It: Shift your perspective on failure and setbacks. See them as opportunities for growth and learning rather than personal shortcomings. Reflect on past failures and extract the lessons and insights they offer. Use these experiences to refine your approach and improve your future performance.
  7. Engage in Self-Validation: Shift your focus from seeking external validation to developing self-validation. Cultivate trust in your instincts and creative vision. Practice self-compassion and acknowledge your progress and achievements. Recognize that your worth and success are not defined solely by the opinions of others.
  8. Create Accountability Structures: Establish accountability structures to support your commitment to turning pro. This could involve finding an accountability partner or joining a mastermind group where members hold each other responsible for their goals and provide support and feedback.
  9. Embrace the Journey of Mastery: Recognize that mastery is a lifelong journey rather than a destination. Embrace the process, setbacks, and challenges that come with it. Cultivate a growth mindset and view each experience as an opportunity for learning and improvement. Celebrate your progress and milestones along the way.
  10. Immerse Yourself in Inspiring Content: Surround yourself with inspirational and motivational content that aligns with your goals and aspirations. Read books, listen to podcasts, or watch documentaries that share stories of individuals who have turned pro and achieved greatness in their fields. Use these resources to fuel your motivation and maintain a positive mindset.

Bear in mind that applying these exercises, techniques, and insights requires consistent effort and dedication. Start with small steps, be patient with yourself, and stay committed to your journey of turning pro and tapping into your inner power to create your life’s work.

Who is this Book Recommended For?

“Turning Pro” is recommended for individuals who aspire to pursue their creative passions, overcome resistance, and unlock their full potential. It is particularly valuable for artists, writers, entrepreneurs, and anyone striving to turn their passion into a career or make a significant impact in their chosen field.

This book is also suitable for individuals seeking personal growth and self-improvement. The concepts and principles explored in “Turning Pro” can be applied to various aspects of life, including relationships, personal development, and achieving a sense of purpose and fulfillment.

Review of the Book

“Turning Pro” offers a compelling and insightful exploration of what it means to embrace professionalism and turn one’s creative aspirations into a reality. Steven Pressfield’s writing style is engaging and thought-provoking, providing practical strategies and examples that readers can easily relate to.

The book excels in its ability to confront the various internal and external obstacles that hinder personal growth and creative expression. It serves as a wake-up call for individuals stuck in the amateur mindset, offering a roadmap for embracing discipline, self-mastery, and the willingness to confront resistance head-on.

But some people may find that the book’s focus on the creative process and artistic pursuits limits its applicability to other areas of life. While the principles presented in “Turning Pro” can be adapted to various fields, individuals seeking specific guidance in non-creative domains may find the book less relevant.

Conclusion

“Turning Pro: Tap Your Inner Power and Create Your Life’s Work” by Steven Pressfield is a powerful and inspiring guide for individuals seeking to overcome resistance, unlock their creative potential, and live a life of purpose and fulfillment. The book offers practical strategies, insights, and examples that resonate with readers and provide a clear roadmap for personal and professional growth.

Pressfield’s exploration of the professional mindset, the journey of mastery, and the importance of embracing challenges and failures offers valuable lessons that extend beyond the realm of creativity. “Turning Pro” is a valuable resource for artists, entrepreneurs, and individuals seeking personal growth and self-improvement.

“Turning Pro” serves as a reminder that turning one’s passion into a profession requires discipline, dedication, and the willingness to confront internal and external obstacles. By embracing professionalism and adopting a pro mindset, individuals can tap into their inner power and create a life’s work that is both fulfilling and impactful.
