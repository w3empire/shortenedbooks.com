Barking Up the Wrong Tree by Eric Barker (Summary & Review)

author Jared Wright
Rating

“Barking Up the Wrong Tree: The Surprising Science Behind Why Everything You Know About Success Is (Mostly) Wrong” by Eric Barker is a book that challenges common assumptions about success.

Barker delves into the science behind what truly drives success and provides readers with practical advice on how to navigate their own paths to achievement. The book explores various aspects of success, including work, relationships, and personal well-being, offering insights backed by research and real-life examples.

Barker’s intention is to debunk popular myths about success and guide readers toward a more effective and fulfilling approach. He draws on a wide range of scientific studies, interviews with experts, and anecdotes from successful individuals to provide a comprehensive exploration of the topic.

By examining conventional wisdom and contrasting it with scientific evidence, Barker encourages readers to question their preconceived notions and adopt strategies that are more likely to lead to genuine success.

Idea 1: Success is Not Always Linked to Grades

Barker challenges the common belief that academic success and high grades directly correlate with real-world success. He emphasizes that success in life often requires a different skill set than what is traditionally valued in educational settings. While academic achievement is important, it does not guarantee success in careers or personal relationships.

Example: Elon Musk, the CEO of Tesla and SpaceX, dropped out of Stanford University to pursue his entrepreneurial ventures. Despite not completing his degree, he went on to become one of the most influential figures in the technology industry.

Practical exercise: Focus on acquiring practical skills and experiences outside the classroom, such as internships, networking, and real-world problem-solving.

Idea 2: Persistence and Grit Matter

Grit can be defined as a person’s ability to persevere and maintain their passion and effort towards a long-term goal, even when faced with challenges, setbacks, or obstacles. It involves having strong determination and resilience to keep going, staying focused, and not giving up easily.

Barker highlights the significance of persistence and grit in achieving long-term success. He argues that resilience in the face of failure, setbacks, and obstacles is a crucial trait possessed by high achievers. Failures don’t demotivate them; rather, they see them as opportunities for learning and are willing to keep trying.

Example: Thomas Edison, the inventor of the light bulb, famously experienced numerous failures before achieving success. He viewed each failure as a step closer to finding the right solution and famously said, “I have not failed. I’ve just found 10,000 ways that won’t work.”

Practical exercise: Develop a growth mindset and embrace challenges. Set goals, persevere through obstacles, and learn from failures.

Idea 3: Networking and Relationships are Key

Barker emphasizes the importance of networking and building meaningful relationships for achieving success. He highlights the role of social capital in opening doors and creating opportunities. Developing a strong network and fostering genuine connections can lead to mentorship, collaboration, and access to valuable resources.

Example: Mark Zuckerberg, the co-founder of Facebook, benefited greatly from his connections with other entrepreneurs and venture capitalists in the early stages of his career. These relationships provided him with the support and guidance necessary to turn his vision into a global phenomenon.

Practical exercise: Attend networking events, join professional organizations, and cultivate relationships with mentors or advisors in their field of interest.

Idea 4: Embrace Discomfort and Take Calculated Risks

Barker suggests that embracing discomfort and taking calculated risks can propel individuals toward success. Stepping outside of one’s comfort zone is often where growth and innovation occur. Playing it safe and avoiding risks may result in stagnation and missed opportunities.

Example: Sara Blakely, the founder of Spanx, took a risk by investing her life savings into launching her innovative shapewear brand. Despite facing initial rejections and setbacks, her willingness to embrace uncertainty paid off, and Spanx became a global success.

Practical exercise: Identify your comfort zones and actively seek opportunities to push beyond them. This could involve volunteering for challenging projects, learning new skills, or pursuing entrepreneurial endeavors.

Idea 5: Focus on Strengths, Not Weaknesses

Barker challenges the notion that success is achieved by fixing weaknesses. Instead, he advocates for identifying and leveraging personal strengths. By focusing on strengths and building upon them, individuals can excel in areas where they naturally thrive.

Example: Warren Buffett, one of the world’s most successful investors, attributes his success to staying within his “circle of competence” and focusing on investments he understands well. By playing to his strengths, he has consistently achieved exceptional results.

Practical exercise: Identify your core strengths and explore ways to apply them in your personal and professional pursuits. This could involve seeking opportunities that align with your natural talents or developing skills that enhance your existing strengths.

Idea 6: Work Smarter, Not Harder

Barker challenges the idea that success is solely a result of working long hours and sacrificing personal well-being. He argues that working smarter, rather than harder, can lead to greater productivity and success. Effective time management, prioritization, and leveraging technology are essential to maximizing efficiency.

Example: Tim Ferriss, the author of “The 4-Hour Workweek,” advocates for outsourcing non-essential tasks, using automation tools, and focusing on high-impact activities. By optimizing his workflow, Ferriss has achieved remarkable success as an author, entrepreneur, and podcast host.

Practical exercise: Analyze your daily routines and identify areas where you can streamline processes, delegate tasks, or leverage technology to increase productivity.

Idea 7: Embrace Failure as a Stepping Stone

Barker challenges the fear of failure and encourages individuals to see it as an integral part of the path to success. Rather than avoiding risks due to the fear of failure, he argues that embracing failure can lead to valuable insights, growth, and resilience.

Example: J.K. Rowling, the author of the Harry Potter series, faced multiple rejections before finding a publisher. Despite setbacks, she persisted and eventually achieved immense success, becoming one of the best-selling authors in history.

Practical exercise: Reflect on past failures and identify the lessons learned from each experience. Develop strategies to approach future challenges with a mindset that embraces the potential for growth and learning.

Idea 8: Happiness Drives Success

Barker challenges the notion that success leads to happiness and argues that it is often the other way around. He presents research showing that happiness and well-being can actually drive success. Positive emotions, resilience, and a healthy mindset contribute to increased productivity, creativity, and overall satisfaction.

Example: Shawn Achor, a positive psychology researcher, conducted studies showing that happier employees are more productive, have better job performance, and experience greater career success.

Practical exercise: Prioritize your well-being by engaging in activities that promote happiness, such as practicing gratitude, exercising, and cultivating positive relationships.

Idea 9: Find Meaning and Purpose

Barker highlights the importance of finding meaning and purpose in one’s work and life. He argues that genuine success is not solely measured by financial or external achievements but also by personal fulfillment and a sense of contribution to something meaningful.

Example: Malala Yousafzai, a Nobel Peace Prize laureate, found purpose in advocating for girls’ education despite facing threats and adversity. Her commitment to her cause has had a significant impact worldwide.

Practical exercise: Take time to reflect on your values, passions, and what gives you a sense of purpose. Explore opportunities and align your goals with your personal values to cultivate a meaningful life.

Idea 10: Continuous Learning and Adaptability

Barker emphasizes the importance of continuous learning and adaptability in a rapidly changing world. He argues that successful individuals are those who embrace a growth mindset, stay curious, and are willing to acquire new knowledge and skills throughout their lives.

Example: Jeff Bezos, the founder of Amazon, prioritizes learning and encourages his employees to adopt a “Day 1” mindset, where they constantly strive to innovate and adapt in a competitive marketplace.

Practical exercise: Set aside time for regular learning and self-improvement. This can involve reading books, attending workshops or webinars, or pursuing formal education or certifications.

Who is this Book Recommended For?

This book is recommended for individuals who are seeking a fresh perspective on success and are open to challenging conventional wisdom.

It is particularly relevant for those who want to understand the science behind success and apply evidence-based strategies in their personal and professional lives.

Students, entrepreneurs, professionals, and anyone interested in personal development and achievement can benefit from the insights provided in this book.

Review of the Book

“Barking Up the Wrong Tree” presents a refreshing and thought-provoking take on success. Eric Barker combines scientific research, real-life examples, and engaging storytelling to challenge commonly held beliefs. The book effectively dismantles myths about success while offering evidence-based strategies to help readers navigate their own paths.

What we liked:

  • The book incorporates a wide range of scientific studies and expert interviews, providing a robust foundation for the ideas presented.
  • Eric Barker’s engaging writing style makes complex concepts accessible and relatable.

What needs improvement:

  • While the book covers a broad range of topics, some readers may find it overwhelming or would prefer a deeper dive into specific areas.

Conclusion

“Barking Up the Wrong Tree” is a compelling and informative book that challenges conventional notions of success. By exploring the science behind achievement and debunking common myths, Eric Barker provides readers with a fresh perspective to navigate their own paths to success.

The book emphasizes the importance of persistence, networking, leveraging strengths, embracing failure, and prioritizing well-being. It underscores the significance of continuous learning, adaptability, and finding meaning and purpose in one’s pursuits.

With its evidence-based approach and engaging storytelling, “Barking Up the Wrong Tree” is a valuable resource for individuals seeking to achieve genuine and lasting success in today’s complex world.
