Resilience by Eric Greitens (Summary & Review)

author Jared Wright
Rating

“Resilience: Hard-Won Wisdom for Living a Better Life” is a self-help book written by Eric Greitens, a former Navy SEAL, humanitarian, and Rhodes Scholar. The book explores the concept of resilience and provides practical advice and insights on how to develop resilience in various aspects of life. Greitens draws from his personal experiences and interviews with individuals who have demonstrated exceptional resilience in the face of adversity.

The main themes of the book revolve around overcoming challenges, embracing failure, taking control of one’s life, and finding purpose. Greitens aims to empower readers to cultivate resilience and lead more meaningful and fulfilling lives. He emphasizes the importance of taking action, embracing discomfort, and developing a resilient mindset to navigate the inevitable hardships that life presents.

Through a combination of personal anecdotes, philosophical insights, and practical exercises, Greitens offers a roadmap for building resilience. He encourages readers to reflect on their values, confront their fears, and develop a sense of purpose that guides their actions. The book provides a holistic approach to resilience, covering various aspects such as mental toughness, emotional well-being, and building strong relationships.

Idea 1: The Resilience Paradigm

Resilience is defined as the capacity to recover quickly from difficulties or adapt in the face of challenges. Greitens explains that resilience is not an innate trait but rather a skill that can be developed through intentional effort. He highlights the importance of cultivating resilience in different areas of life, including personal relationships, professional endeavors, and facing adversity.

To develop resilience, Greitens suggests embracing discomfort and adversity rather than avoiding them. He encourages readers to view setbacks and failures as opportunities for growth and learning. By reframing challenges as stepping stones rather than obstacles, you can cultivate resilience and develop the ability to bounce back stronger from adversity.

Example: Imagine you are facing a challenging project at work that seems overwhelming. Instead of succumbing to stress and fear, you can view it as an opportunity to develop your problem-solving skills and expand your capabilities. Embracing the discomfort and adopting a resilient mindset will help you navigate the challenges and emerge stronger.

Practical exercise: Take a moment to reflect on a recent setback or failure you experienced. Write down three lessons or insights you gained from that experience. Consider how those lessons can contribute to your personal growth and resilience moving forward.

Idea 2: From Failure to Triumph

Failure is an inevitable part of life, and Greitens emphasizes the importance of embracing failure as a stepping stone to success. He argues that avoiding failure and playing it safe can lead to a stagnant and unfulfilling life. Instead, he encourages readers to take risks, pursue their passions, and embrace failure as a necessary part of the journey toward personal growth and success.

Greitens shares stories of individuals who have faced significant failures but managed to bounce back and achieve remarkable success. He highlights the resilience they displayed in the face of adversity and encourages readers to adopt a similar mindset.

Example: Thomas Edison, the inventor of the light bulb, famously stated, “I have not failed. I’ve just found 10,000 ways that won’t work.” Edison’s persistence and ability to view failure as feedback rather than defeat were instrumental in his ultimate success. Embracing failure as a natural part of the learning process allows you to persevere and iterate until you achieve your goals.

Practical exercise: Identify a goal or aspiration that you’ve been hesitant to pursue due to fear of failure. Write down three steps you can take to overcome your fear and start working towards that goal. Embrace the possibility of failure and commit to learning and growing from the experience.

Idea 3: Transforming Emotions through Behavior

Greitens emphasizes the connection between actions and emotions, suggesting that you have the power to influence how you feel by changing your behaviors. He encourages readers to take proactive steps toward positive change, even when they don’t feel motivated or confident.

Rather than waiting for motivation to strike, Greitens suggests adopting a “do it anyway” mentality. By taking action, even in small ways, you can create positive momentum and gradually shift your emotions and mindset. He emphasizes the importance of consistency and discipline in developing resilient habits.

Example: If you’re feeling demotivated or down, you can choose to engage in activities that align with your values and goals. This could involve going for a run, practicing a hobby, or reaching out to a friend for support. Taking action, even when you don’t feel like it, can create a positive ripple effect and enhance your overall well-being.

Practical exercise: Identify an activity that you know has a positive impact on your mood or well-being, even if you’re not currently motivated to do it. Commit to engaging in that activity for at least 10 minutes each day for the next week. Notice how your emotions and motivation levels shift as a result of taking consistent action.

Idea 4: Taming the Inner Demons

Greitens explores the concept of self-discipline and its role in building resilience. He argues that self-discipline is the key to overcoming destructive habits and impulses that hinder personal growth. By mastering self-discipline, you can make deliberate choices aligned with your values and long-term goals.

He offers practical strategies for developing self-discipline, such as setting clear boundaries, creating accountability systems, and cultivating self-awareness. Greitens emphasizes the importance of understanding your triggers and temptations and implementing strategies to overcome them.

Example: If you struggle with procrastination, you can set specific deadlines for tasks and hold yourself accountable by sharing your goals with a trusted friend or using productivity apps. By creating a structured environment and implementing accountability measures, you can develop self-discipline and overcome self-sabotaging behaviors.

Practical exercise: Identify one area in your life where you would like to develop more self-discipline. Set specific, achievable goals related to that area, and create a plan to hold yourself accountable. Consider implementing strategies such as rewards, reminders, or accountability partners to support your progress.

Idea 5: The Breath of Resilience

Greitens explores the power of breath as a tool for cultivating resilience and managing stress. He introduces various breathing techniques and highlights their ability to regulate emotions, enhance focus, and promote overall well-being. By harnessing the power of your breath, you can gain control over your physiological and emotional responses.

He explains how intentional breathing can help you navigate challenging situations, reduce anxiety, and improve decision-making. Greitens encourages readers to incorporate breathwork into their daily routines as a means of developing resilience and finding inner calm.

Example: Before an important presentation or performance, you can engage in a deep breathing exercise to calm your nerves and center your focus. By taking slow, deliberate breaths, you activate the body’s relaxation response and create a sense of calm and stability.

Practical exercise: Take a few minutes each day to practice deep breathing. Find a quiet space, sit comfortably, and inhale deeply through your nose, allowing your belly to expand. Exhale slowly through your mouth, releasing any tension or stress. Repeat this process for several minutes, focusing on the sensation of breath entering and leaving your body.

Idea 6: Crafting a Life of Meaning

Greitens challenges the notion of finding one’s purpose and instead suggests that purpose is something that individuals must actively create through their actions and values. He encourages readers to reflect on their core values and use them as a guiding compass to shape their actions and decisions.

He emphasizes the importance of service and finding meaning in contributing to something greater than oneself. By aligning your actions with your values and engaging in acts of service, you can cultivate a sense of purpose and fulfillment.

Example: Rather than waiting for a grand revelation or external validation of your purpose, you can start by identifying the values that matter most to you. Consider how you can embody those values in your daily life, whether through acts of kindness, volunteering, or pursuing meaningful work. By living in alignment with your values, you actively create a sense of purpose.

Practical exercise: Take some time to reflect on your core values and write them down. Consider how you can incorporate these values into your daily life and make choices that align with them. Identify one small action you can take today that embodies one of your core values, and commit to carrying it out.

Who is this Book Recommended For?

“Resilience: Hard-Won Wisdom for Living a Better Life” is recommended for individuals who are seeking guidance and inspiration to develop resilience and overcome challenges in various aspects of life. The book is particularly relevant for those who find themselves stuck in negative patterns, seeking to navigate setbacks, and looking to cultivate a sense of purpose and fulfillment.

This book would benefit individuals who are open to introspection, willing to embrace discomfort, and eager to take action. It provides practical strategies and exercises that can be applied to personal and professional life, making it valuable for individuals from diverse backgrounds and age groups.

Review of the Book and Conclusion

“Resilience: Hard-Won Wisdom for Living a Better Life” is a practical guide for developing resilience and finding meaning in the face of adversity. Eric Greitens combines personal anecdotes, philosophical insights, and actionable advice to provide readers with a comprehensive roadmap for building resilience in various areas of life.

The author challenges readers to step out of their comfort zones, embrace failure, and actively create a purpose-driven life. The practical exercises provided throughout the book enable readers to apply the concepts and principles to their own lives, fostering personal growth and resilience.

In summary, “Resilience” offers valuable insights and actionable strategies for anyone seeking to cultivate resilience, overcome challenges, and lead a more purposeful life. By embracing the wisdom and practical advice shared by Eric Greitens, readers can embark on a transformative journey of personal growth and empowerment.
