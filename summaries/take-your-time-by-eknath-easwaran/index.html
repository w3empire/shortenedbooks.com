Take Your Time Summary of Key Ideas and Review - Eknath Easwaran

author Dean Stewart
Rating

“Take Your Time: The Wisdom of Slowing Down” is a thought-provoking book written by Eknath Easwaran, a renowned spiritual teacher and author. The book serves as a guide to help individuals navigate the fast-paced modern world and discover the transformative power of slowing down. Easwaran emphasizes the importance of taking time for oneself, cultivating inner peace, and living with mindfulness and intention.

Easwaran’s main intention is to provide readers with practical tools and insights to counter the prevailing culture of speed and constant busyness. He encourages readers to embrace a slower pace of life, not as a means of procrastination or laziness but as a way to connect with their true selves, find meaning, and experience profound joy.

Through engaging anecdotes, personal stories, and timeless wisdom from various traditions, Easwaran offers guidance on how to slow down and live a more balanced and fulfilling life.

The book explores themes such as mindfulness, meditation, self-awareness, simplicity, and the importance of solitude. Easwaran draws upon his own experiences as well as teachings from spiritual masters, philosophers, and poets to convey the significance of these concepts in today’s hectic world.

By urging readers to prioritize self-care, reflection, and deep listening, he illuminates the path to a more meaningful and joyful existence.

Idea 1: Embracing the Present Moment

The first main idea explores the importance of embracing the present moment. Easwaran highlights how our tendency to dwell in the past or anxiously anticipate the future robs us of fully experiencing the richness of the present. He emphasizes the need to cultivate mindfulness, the practice of intentionally paying attention to the present moment without judgment.

By grounding yourself in the present, you become more aware of your thoughts, emotions, and sensations. This heightened awareness allows you to savor life’s simple pleasures, such as the taste of food, the beauty of nature, or the warmth of a loved one’s embrace.

It also enables you to respond more skillfully to challenges and conflicts, as you can observe your reactions and choose how to act rather than being driven by unconscious patterns.

Practical exercise: Take a few moments each day to engage in a simple mindfulness practice. Sit comfortably, close your eyes, and bring your attention to your breath. Notice the sensation of the breath as it enters and leaves your body. Whenever your mind wanders, gently bring it back to your breath. This exercise helps anchor you in the present moment and cultivate a sense of calm and clarity.

Example: Imagine you are sipping a cup of tea. Instead of rushing through the experience, take your time to fully engage with the present moment. Notice the aroma, the temperature, and the taste of the tea. Allow yourself to be fully present, savoring each sip without distractions. By doing so, you can experience a deep sense of contentment and gratitude.

Idea 2: Cultivating Inner Stillness

In the midst of a fast-paced world, cultivating inner stillness becomes essential for well-being and clarity of mind. Easwaran emphasizes the value of taking regular breaks from external stimulation and creating space for solitude and silence. These moments of stillness allow you to recharge, reconnect with yourself, and tap into your inner wisdom.

By intentionally setting aside time for solitude, you create an opportunity to reflect, introspect, and gain insight into your thoughts and emotions. This practice of self-inquiry helps you develop a deeper understanding of yourself and your values, enabling you to make choices aligned with your authentic desires. It also fosters a sense of inner peace and resilience, providing a refuge amidst the chaos of everyday life.

Practical exercise: Dedicate a specific time each day to be alone and engage in a quiet activity that brings you joy and relaxation. It could be reading a book, going for a walk in nature, or practicing meditation. During this time, resist the urge to fill the silence with external distractions and instead allow yourself to simply be present with your own thoughts and feelings.

Example: Picture yourself sitting in a peaceful park, surrounded by the sounds of nature. Take a few moments to soak in the tranquility of the environment. Notice how the stillness envelops you, allowing your mind to settle and your body to relax. As you immerse yourself in this moment of solitude, you feel a deep sense of calm and connectedness.

Idea 3: Finding Joy in Simplicity

Amidst the consumerist culture that constantly promotes accumulation and external achievements, Easwaran invites readers to embrace the beauty of simplicity. He encourages individuals to reassess their priorities and let go of the pursuit of material possessions and status symbols, which often lead to dissatisfaction and stress.

Simplicity, according to Easwaran, involves decluttering not just our physical surroundings but also our minds and schedules. By simplifying our lives, we create space for what truly matters, such as meaningful relationships, personal growth, and experiences that nourish the soul. This shift in focus from external achievements to inner fulfillment allows us to find joy in the present moment and appreciate the abundance that already exists in our lives.

Practical exercise: Take inventory of your belongings and identify items that no longer serve a purpose or bring you joy. Donate or discard these possessions, creating a sense of spaciousness and lightness in your environment. Also, examine your commitments and activities, and consider letting go of those that no longer align with your values or bring you fulfillment.

Example: Reflect on a time when you engaged in a simple activity that brought you deep joy and contentment. It could be spending quality time with loved ones, engaging in a creative pursuit, or connecting with nature. Recall the feelings of satisfaction and inner peace that arose from the simplicity of that experience. By embracing simplicity in your daily life, you can cultivate more of these moments of profound joy.

Idea 4: Nurturing the Power of Patience

In a world that thrives on instant gratification and constant productivity, patience has become a scarce virtue. Easwaran highlights the transformative power of cultivating patience as an antidote to stress, impulsiveness, and dissatisfaction. Patience allows you to navigate challenges with grace, embrace uncertainty, and cultivate a deeper sense of trust and resilience.

By practicing patience, you learn to accept things as they are, recognizing that some processes and outcomes take time. This perspective frees you from unnecessary suffering caused by impatience and resistance. It also opens up the possibility of finding hidden blessings and opportunities in moments of waiting or setbacks.

Practical exercise: Choose a task or activity that typically tests your patience, such as waiting in a long line or dealing with a technical issue. Before engaging in the task, set the intention to approach it with patience and acceptance. When impatience arises, take a deep breath, remind yourself of the benefits of patience, and consciously choose to respond with calmness and understanding.

Example: Imagine you are stuck in a traffic jam, running late for an important appointment. Instead of becoming frustrated and agitated, use the opportunity to practice patience. Take a deep breath, relax your body, and remind yourself that you have no control over the traffic. Shift your focus to something positive, such as listening to an inspiring podcast or enjoying the scenery around you. By cultivating patience in such situations, you can transform moments of frustration into opportunities for inner growth.

Idea 5: Prioritizing Self-Care

In the relentless pursuit of external goals and obligations, self-care often takes a backseat. Easwaran emphasizes the crucial role of self-care in maintaining overall well-being and living a balanced life. He encourages readers to prioritize self-care as an act of self-respect and self-compassion.

Self-care involves nourishing your physical, emotional, and spiritual needs. It encompasses activities such as getting adequate rest, engaging in regular exercise, eating nourishing foods, engaging in hobbies, seeking support from loved ones, and setting boundaries to protect your time and energy. By prioritizing self-care, you replenish your inner resources, enhance your resilience, and create a solid foundation for living a meaningful and purposeful life.

Practical exercise: Create a self-care routine that includes activities that nourish different aspects of your well-being. Set aside dedicated time each day for self-care, and commit to honoring these moments as non-negotiable. It could involve taking a warm bath, journaling, practicing yoga or meditation, or engaging in creative expressions that bring you joy.

Example: Imagine you have had a busy day filled with various responsibilities and deadlines. Instead of continuing to push through exhaustion, prioritize self-care by allocating time to rest and recharge. Take a warm bath, engage in a relaxing activity such as reading or listening to soothing music, and give yourself permission to unwind. By honoring your need for self-care, you replenish your energy and ensure that you show up as your best self in all areas of your life.

Idea 6: The Art of Deep Listening

In a world characterized by constant noise and distractions, the art of deep listening has become a rare and valuable skill. Easwaran emphasizes the transformative power of attentive listening in building meaningful connections, fostering empathy, and cultivating a sense of presence.

Deep listening involves not only hearing the words spoken by others but also paying attention to their underlying emotions, body language, and unspoken messages. It requires setting aside personal judgments and agendas and fully immersing yourself in the present moment. By practicing deep listening, you create a safe space for others to express themselves authentically, feel seen and heard, and deepen your understanding of their experiences.

Practical exercise: Choose a conversation or interaction in your daily life and make a conscious effort to practice deep listening. Focus your attention fully on the person speaking, maintain eye contact, and resist the urge to interrupt or formulate a response. Instead, allow yourself to be fully present and attentive to their words, emotions, and nonverbal cues. After the conversation, reflect on how deep listening enhanced your connection and understanding.

Example: Imagine you are having a conversation with a close friend who is going through a challenging time. Instead of offering advice or attempting to fix their problems, practice deep listening. Give them your undivided attention, maintain eye contact, and create a safe space for them to share their thoughts and feelings. By genuinely listening and validating their experiences, you provide them with the support and empathy they need.

Idea 7: Living with Intention

In a world driven by external expectations and societal norms, living with intention becomes a powerful tool for personal empowerment and fulfillment. Easwaran invites readers to reflect on their core values, aspirations, and deepest desires and align their actions with their inner truth.

Living with intention involves consciously choosing how you spend your time, energy, and resources. It means setting clear intentions and goals that resonate with your authentic self and taking deliberate steps towards their realization. By living in alignment with your values and aspirations, you cultivate a sense of purpose, fulfillment, and authenticity in all aspects of your life.

Practical exercise: Set aside time for self-reflection and introspection. Journal about your values, dreams, and the legacy you wish to leave behind. Based on these insights, identify one or two areas of your life where you can align your actions more intentionally. Set clear intentions for these areas and develop an action plan to support your intentions.

Example: Suppose one of your core values is environmental sustainability. Living with intention in this area may involve making conscious choices such as reducing your carbon footprint, consuming mindfully, and supporting eco-friendly initiatives. By aligning your actions with your values, you contribute to a cause that is meaningful to you and experience a deep sense of purpose and fulfillment.

Idea 8: The Power of Compassion

In a world that often values competition and individualism, cultivating compassion becomes essential for creating a more harmonious and interconnected society. Easwaran emphasizes the transformative power of compassion in fostering empathy, understanding, and unity.

Compassion involves recognizing the inherent worth and dignity of every individual and responding with kindness, empathy, and care. It starts with cultivating self-compassion, extending love and understanding to oneself, and then radiates outward to include others. By practicing compassion, you foster a sense of interconnectedness, reduce conflicts, and contribute to the well-being of both yourself and those around you.

Practical exercise: Engage in a compassion meditation practice. Find a comfortable position, close your eyes, and bring to mind someone you care deeply about. Hold them in your awareness and silently repeat phrases such as “May you be happy, may you be healthy, may you be safe, and may you live with ease.” Extend this practice to include yourself, loved ones, acquaintances, and even those with whom you may have difficulties.

Example: Imagine encountering a stranger who appears upset or distressed. Instead of turning away or dismissing their emotions, practice compassion. Approach them with kindness, listen empathetically, and offer words of support and encouragement. By practicing compassion in this way, you contribute to their well-being and create a ripple effect of kindness and understanding.

Idea 9: Cultivating Gratitude

In a world that often focuses on what is lacking or missing, cultivating gratitude becomes a powerful practice for shifting our perspective and cultivating joy and contentment. Easwaran encourages readers to develop a gratitude mindset, wherein we consciously appreciate the blessings, big and small, that exist in our lives.

Gratitude involves acknowledging and expressing appreciation for the abundance that surrounds us. It can be directed towards relationships, experiences, nature, achievements, or even challenges that offer opportunities for growth. By cultivating gratitude, we shift our attention from what is lacking to what is present, fostering a sense of joy, connection, and abundance.

Practical exercise: Start a gratitude journal, dedicating a few minutes each day to write down three things you are grateful for. They can be simple things like a warm cup of coffee, a kind gesture from a friend, or a beautiful sunset. Engage your senses and emotions as you reflect on each item, truly savoring the experience of gratitude.

Example: Imagine waking up in the morning and expressing gratitude for the gift of a new day. Take a moment to appreciate the sunlight streaming through your window, the comfort of your bed, and the opportunities that lie ahead. By cultivating gratitude in this way, you start your day with a positive mindset and set the tone for a joyful and fulfilling experience.

Idea 10: Embracing Impermanence

In a world characterized by constant change, embracing impermanence becomes essential for navigating life’s ups and downs with grace and resilience. Easwaran invites readers to recognize the transient nature of all things and to let go of attachments and expectations.

Embracing impermanence involves accepting that everything is in a constant state of flux, including our thoughts, emotions, relationships, and circumstances. By cultivating this awareness, we develop a sense of detachment and non-attachment, freeing ourselves from clinging to outcomes and embracing the present moment as it unfolds.

Practical exercise: Engage in a contemplation on impermanence. Reflect on the fact that everything in life is subject to change, including your own body, thoughts, and emotions. Allow yourself to embrace the truth of impermanence and the opportunities it presents for growth, transformation, and the cultivation of gratitude.

Example: Picture yourself walking along the seashore, observing the waves crashing onto the shore. Observe the constant ebb and flow and the ever-changing patterns and shapes. As you witness this natural rhythm of impermanence, allow yourself to let go of any attachments or expectations and find peace in the acceptance of change.

Who is this Book Recommended For?

“Take Your Time: The Wisdom of Slowing Down” is recommended for individuals who feel overwhelmed, stressed, or disconnected in today’s fast-paced world. It is particularly valuable for those seeking guidance on finding meaning and fulfillment amidst the chaos of modern life. The book offers practical tools and insights that can be applied by people of all backgrounds, regardless of their religious or spiritual beliefs.

This book is especially beneficial for individuals who are interested in mindfulness, meditation, personal growth, and living with intention. It provides a roadmap for slowing down, cultivating inner peace, and embracing a more balanced and meaningful existence. Whether you are a busy professional, a student, a parent, or someone simply seeking a deeper connection with yourself and the world around you, “Take Your Time” offers valuable wisdom and practical guidance.

Review of the Book and Conclusion

“Take Your Time: The Wisdom of Slowing Down” is a profound and insightful book that offers a refreshing perspective on living a meaningful and balanced life in a fast-paced world. Eknath Easwaran’s wisdom and guidance serve as a gentle reminder to prioritize self-care, cultivate mindfulness, and embrace the power of slowing down.

The book excels in its ability to convey complex concepts in a clear and accessible manner, making it suitable for readers of all backgrounds. Easwaran’s use of engaging anecdotes, personal stories, and timeless wisdom from various traditions adds depth and relatability to the teachings. 

But one thing that could be improved upon is the inclusion of more diverse perspectives and experiences. While the book draws from various spiritual and philosophical traditions, a broader range of voices and stories could enhance its relevance and resonance for a wider audience.

In conclusion, “Take Your Time” is an excellent reference for anyone seeking to slow down, find inner peace, and live with intention in a world that often values speed and external achievements.

It offers timeless wisdom and practical guidance that can empower readers to create a more meaningful, fulfilling, and balanced life. By embracing the wisdom of slowing down, you can cultivate a deep sense of joy, authenticity, and connectedness in every moment.
