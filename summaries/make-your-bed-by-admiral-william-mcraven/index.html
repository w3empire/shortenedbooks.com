Make Your Bed by Admiral William McRaven (Summary & Review)

author Dean Stewart
Rating

“Make Your Bed” is a motivational book written by Admiral William H. McRaven, a retired U.S. Navy SEAL and former commander of U.S. Special Operations Command. The book draws inspiration from Admiral McRaven’s commencement speech at the University of Texas at Austin in 2014, where he shared ten life lessons he learned during his Navy SEAL training.

The central theme of the book revolves around the idea that small, seemingly insignificant actions can have a profound impact on an individual’s life and the world at large.

Through personal anecdotes and experiences, Admiral McRaven emphasizes the importance of discipline, perseverance, and attention to detail. He shares stories from his SEAL training and military career to illustrate how simple acts, such as making your bed in the morning, can set the tone for a successful day and create a sense of accomplishment.

The book’s primary aim is to inspire readers to embrace these small actions and use them as stepping stones to achieve bigger goals.

Idea 1: Start Your Day with a Task Completed

Admiral McRaven asserts that by making your bed each morning, you start your day with a task completed. This small act can provide a sense of pride and motivation to tackle other tasks throughout the day.

It sets a positive tone and gives you a small win early on, boosting your productivity and confidence. Making your bed serves as a metaphor for accomplishing tasks in all aspects of life. It reinforces the idea that taking care of the little things can lead to greater achievements.

Idea 2: Embrace the Power of Teamwork

Teamwork is a crucial aspect of Navy SEAL training, and Admiral McRaven highlights its significance in the book. He emphasizes the importance of relying on others, building strong relationships, and understanding that success is rarely achieved alone.

McRaven believes that by working together and supporting one another, individuals can overcome challenges and achieve extraordinary results. He encourages readers to foster a spirit of collaboration in their personal and professional lives, recognizing that collective effort can lead to greater impact and success.

Idea 3: Get Comfortable with Failure and Learn from It

Admiral McRaven acknowledges that failure is an inevitable part of life, but it shouldn’t define or discourage individuals. He recounts instances where his SEAL teammates and he faced setbacks and learned valuable lessons from those experiences.

McRaven encourages readers to embrace failure as an opportunity for growth and learning. He suggests that by maintaining a positive attitude, analyzing failures, and adapting one’s approach, individuals can overcome obstacles and ultimately achieve success.

Idea 4: Dare to Take Risks and Step Outside Your Comfort Zone

Admiral McRaven advocates for taking calculated risks and venturing beyond one’s comfort zone. He believes that growth and progress occur when individuals push their boundaries and embrace challenges.

McRaven shares personal anecdotes from his military career, highlighting instances where he had to step into unfamiliar territory and face daunting tasks. By challenging ourselves and embracing discomfort, we develop resilience, expand our capabilities, and unlock our full potential.

Idea 5: Never Give Up, Even in the Face of Adversity

In the book, Admiral McRaven stresses the importance of perseverance and resilience. He shares stories of Navy SEAL training, where candidates face grueling physical and mental challenges. Despite the demanding circumstances, those who persisted and refused to give up ultimately succeeded.

McRaven encourages readers to adopt a similar mindset and to maintain determination when facing adversity. He emphasizes that setbacks should not deter individuals from pursuing their goals and that success often comes to those who persevere.

Idea 6: Pay Attention to the “Sugar Cookies”

Admiral McRaven introduces the concept of “sugar cookies” as a metaphor for embracing the unexpected obstacles that arise in life. In SEAL training, “sugar cookies” referred to the recruits who were ordered to roll in the sand until they were covered from head to toe.

This experience taught them the importance of embracing discomfort and finding humor in challenging situations. McRaven suggests that by reframing difficult circumstances and maintaining a positive attitude, individuals can transform setbacks into opportunities for growth and personal development.

Idea 7: Stay Focused on Your Immediate Mission

Throughout the book, Admiral McRaven underscores the significance of staying focused on the present moment and the task at hand. In SEAL training, the candidates are taught to focus on their immediate mission and not get overwhelmed by the enormity of their long-term goals.

McRaven suggests that by breaking down larger goals into smaller, manageable tasks, individuals can maintain focus and steadily progress toward their objectives. This approach helps avoid feeling overwhelmed and fosters a sense of control and accomplishment.

Idea 8: Stand Up to Bullies and Show Respect for Others

Admiral McRaven shares stories from his childhood and military training to emphasize the importance of standing up to bullies and treating others with respect and dignity. He advocates for creating a culture of inclusivity and rejecting any form of discrimination or mistreatment.

The author believes that by taking a stand against injustice and treating others with kindness, individuals can contribute to positive change and make the world a better place.

Idea 9: Give Your Best Effort in Everything You Do

Admiral McRaven emphasizes the significance of giving one’s best effort in every endeavor. He argues that by striving for excellence and maintaining high standards, individuals develop a strong work ethic and establish a reputation for dependability and quality.

McRaven encourages readers to approach even the smallest tasks with dedication and enthusiasm, as these actions can influence the outcome of more significant undertakings. He asserts that the habits formed through consistently giving one’s best effort have a cumulative effect and lead to long-term success.

Idea 10: Never, Ever Ring the Bell

In SEAL training, a brass bell symbolizes giving up. Candidates who can’t endure the rigorous training have the option to ring the bell, signaling their resignation. Admiral McRaven stresses the importance of never ringing the bell in life, symbolizing the commitment to never give up, no matter the circumstances.

He inspires readers to persevere, remain steadfast in their goals, and maintain the resolve to overcome challenges. By adopting a mindset of resilience and determination, individuals can achieve greatness.

Bonus Section: 15 Practical Exercises and Techniques to Transform Your Life, Inspired by “Make Your Bed”

Here is a list of exercises and techniques based on the principles discussed in “Make Your Bed: Little Things That Can Change Your Life…And Maybe the World”:

  1. Make Your Bed: Start each day by making your bed. This simple act creates a sense of accomplishment and sets a positive tone for the rest of the day.
  2. Set Small Goals: Break down your larger goals into smaller, manageable tasks. Focus on completing these tasks one by one, celebrating each accomplishment along the way.
  3. Practice Teamwork: Seek opportunities to collaborate with others. Engage in group projects or join a team where you can contribute your skills and work together towards a common objective.
  4. Embrace Failure: Change your perspective on failure. View it as an opportunity to learn and grow, rather than a setback. Reflect on past failures and identify the lessons you can take from them.
  5. Step Outside Your Comfort Zone: Challenge yourself to try new things and take calculated risks. Engage in activities that push your boundaries and allow you to develop new skills and perspectives.
  6. Develop Resilience: When faced with adversity, remind yourself to never give up. Maintain a positive attitude, stay focused on your goals, and find ways to adapt and overcome obstacles.
  7. Pay Attention to Detail: Practice paying attention to the small details in your tasks and interactions. Take pride in your workmanship and strive for excellence in everything you do.
  8. Stand Up for Others: Be an advocate for kindness and respect. Stand up against injustice, support those who are being mistreated, and create a culture of inclusivity and compassion.
  9. Give Your Best Effort: Approach each task with dedication and enthusiasm. Strive for excellence in all areas of your life, even seemingly insignificant tasks. Develop a strong work ethic and establish a reputation for dependability.
  10. Maintain Focus: Train yourself to stay present and focused on the task at hand. Break larger projects into smaller, manageable steps and concentrate on completing each step before moving on to the next.
  11. Practice Positive Self-Talk: Develop a habit of positive self-talk and affirmations. Replace self-doubt and negative thoughts with encouraging and empowering statements.
  12. Create a Morning Routine: Establish a morning routine that includes activities that set a positive tone for your day. Consider incorporating exercises, meditation, journaling, or reading uplifting materials.
  13. Seek Mentorship: Find mentors or role models who exemplify the qualities and values you admire. Learn from their experiences and seek guidance and support as you work towards your goals.
  14. Celebrate Progress: Acknowledge and celebrate your achievements, both big and small. Take time to reflect on how far you’ve come and the milestones you’ve reached.
  15. Never Give Up: Adopt the mindset of never giving up, even when faced with challenges or setbacks. Remind yourself of the importance of perseverance and the long-term benefits it can bring.

These exercises and techniques based on the principles of “Make Your Bed” can help you develop discipline, resilience, and a positive mindset. Incorporate them into your daily life to create positive habits and work towards achieving your goals.

Who is this Book Recommended For?

“Make Your Bed” is recommended for individuals who seek motivation and inspiration to overcome challenges and achieve personal and professional success. The book appeals to those who value discipline, teamwork, and a positive mindset.

It is particularly relevant to individuals interested in military leadership principles and how they can be applied in various aspects of life. Readers who appreciate personal anecdotes and stories of triumph in the face of adversity will find the book engaging and relatable.

Review of the Book

“Make Your Bed” offers a concise and motivational perspective on the power of small actions and mindset in achieving success. Admiral McRaven’s personal experiences and stories provide credibility and make the book relatable.

The ideas presented are straightforward and practical, offering readers tangible takeaways to implement in their own lives. The book’s concise format and easy-to-understand language make it accessible to a wide range of readers.

But keep in mind that some readers may find the book’s content to be relatively familiar, as the concepts discussed are not entirely groundbreaking or novel. Some ideas could benefit from more in-depth exploration and analysis, as they are presented more as anecdotes than fully developed concepts.

Moreover, while the book focuses on individual actions and mindsets, it could have delved further into the systemic factors that contribute to success or hinder progress.

Conclusion

“Make Your Bed” by Admiral William McRaven provides valuable insights into the power of small actions, discipline, and resilience in achieving personal and professional success. By sharing his experiences and lessons from Navy SEAL training, McRaven offers practical advice that readers can implement in their own lives.

The book serves as a reminder that by embracing the little things, maintaining a positive mindset, and persevering through challenges, individuals can make a meaningful impact and change the world for the better.
