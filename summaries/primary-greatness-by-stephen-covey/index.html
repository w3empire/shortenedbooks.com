Primary Greatness by Stephen Covey (Summary & Review)

author Carlos Valentino
Rating

“Primary Greatness: The 12 Levers of Success” is a self-help book written by Stephen Covey, the renowned author of “The 7 Habits of Highly Effective People.” In this book, Covey explores the fundamental principles and values that lead to true success and fulfillment in life. He emphasizes the importance of aligning our actions and choices with these principles to unlock our primary greatness.

Covey’s intention with this book is to guide readers towards a deeper understanding of what it means to live a successful and meaningful life. He presents twelve core principles, or “levers,” that serve as foundational elements for personal and professional growth. By embracing and applying these principles, individuals can cultivate their character, relationships, and contributions to the world.

Through engaging stories and thought-provoking insights, Covey encourages readers to reflect on their own lives and consider how they can integrate these principles into their daily existence. The book is designed to empower readers to take responsibility for their choices, make a positive impact on others, and create a life of significance.

Lever 1: The Lever of Integrity

The first lever Covey explores is the Lever of Integrity. Integrity involves living in alignment with our deeply held values and principles. It means being honest, reliable, and trustworthy in all aspects of life. Covey highlights that integrity is not just about following rules but about making choices that are congruent with our beliefs and moral compass.

Integrity requires self-awareness and the willingness to make difficult decisions when faced with ethical dilemmas. It means taking responsibility for our actions and holding ourselves accountable for the consequences. Covey emphasizes that integrity is not a one-time act but a lifelong commitment to living with honesty and authenticity.

Example: Imagine you’re working on a project at your workplace, and your team is facing a deadline. However, you discover a mistake that could potentially harm the project’s quality. Acting with integrity, you choose to bring the issue to your team’s attention and work together to find a solution, even if it means extending the deadline. Your integrity strengthens your professional relationships and builds trust within the team.

Practical exercise: Take some time to reflect on your core values and principles. Identify areas in your life where you may be compromising your integrity and commit to making changes. Consider the impact of your choices on others and strive to align your actions consistently with your values.

Lever 2: The Lever of Contribution

The second lever Covey discusses is the Lever of Contribution. Contribution refers to the act of making a positive difference in the lives of others and the world at large. Covey emphasizes that true success is not measured solely by personal achievements but by the impact we have on others.

Contribution can take various forms, including sharing knowledge, supporting others, volunteering, or using one’s skills and resources to address societal issues. Covey emphasizes that when we shift our focus from self-centeredness to service, we experience a deeper sense of purpose and fulfillment in life.

Example: Consider a successful entrepreneur who uses their wealth and influence to create educational programs for underprivileged children. Through their contribution, they provide opportunities for those who may not have otherwise had access to quality education. Their generosity and commitment to making a difference in the lives of others elevate their success beyond their financial achievements.

Practical exercise: Reflect on the ways you can contribute to the well-being of others. Identify your unique skills, resources, or areas of expertise that can be used to make a positive impact. Start small by seeking opportunities to volunteer, mentor, or support a cause you care about. As you contribute, observe how it enriches your life and the lives of those around you.

Lever 3: The Lever of Priority

The third lever Covey explores is the Lever of Priority. Priority is about identifying and focusing on what truly matters in our lives. It involves clarifying our values, setting meaningful goals, and aligning our actions accordingly. Covey emphasizes that when we prioritize effectively, we make conscious choices that lead to personal and professional fulfillment.

Priority requires introspection and the ability to differentiate between urgent and important tasks. Covey introduces the concept of the “big rocks,” representing the most significant aspects of our lives, such as relationships, personal growth, health, and meaningful work. By placing these big rocks first and organizing our time and energy around them, we can avoid getting caught up in trivial matters and invest in what truly brings us joy and fulfillment.

Example: Imagine you have a hectic work schedule and a long to-do list. However, you prioritize spending quality time with your family as a “big rock.” You proactively plan family activities, set boundaries around work, and ensure that your loved ones feel valued and supported. By making family a priority, you create a harmonious balance between your personal and professional lives.

Practical exercise: Take some time to identify your big rocks—the areas of your life that are most important to you. Reflect on how you can allocate your time and resources to prioritize these areas. Create a daily or weekly schedule that reflects your priorities, and be intentional about dedicating time to what truly matters.

Lever 4: The Lever of Sacrifice

The fourth lever Covey explores is the Lever of Sacrifice. Sacrifice refers to willingly giving up something of value for a greater purpose or principle. Covey highlights that true success often requires making sacrifices and letting go of immediate gratification for long-term fulfillment.

Sacrifice involves making choices that align with our values and goals, even if they require short-term discomfort or inconvenience. It requires discipline, self-control, and a clear understanding of what we are willing to give up in order to achieve our desired outcomes. Covey emphasizes that sacrificing lesser things for greater things is a crucial aspect of personal growth and success.

Example: Imagine you have a goal to improve your physical health and fitness. Sacrifice comes into play when you choose to wake up early every morning to exercise instead of hitting the snooze button and staying in bed. You willingly sacrifice the comfort of sleeping in for the long-term benefits of a healthier body and increased energy levels.

Practical exercise: Reflect on your long-term goals and aspirations. Identify areas in your life where you may need to make sacrifices to align with those goals. Determine what you are willing to give up in terms of time, comfort, or certain habits. Develop a plan to make those sacrifices and commit to them, keeping your long-term vision in mind.

Lever 5: The Lever of Service

The fifth lever Covey discusses is the Lever of Service. Service involves a selfless focus on meeting the needs of others and making a positive difference in their lives. Covey emphasizes that by shifting our mindset from self-centeredness to service, we create meaningful connections, build strong relationships, and contribute to the greater good.

Service can be expressed in various contexts, such as in our personal relationships, our communities, or our workplaces. It involves actively listening, empathizing, and offering support to others. Covey highlights the importance of considering the impact of our actions on those around us and seeking opportunities to be of service in our daily interactions.

Example: Consider a manager who genuinely cares about the well-being and growth of their team members. They regularly check in with their employees, provide mentorship, and create an environment that fosters professional development. By serving their team, the manager not only builds a loyal and motivated workforce but also cultivates a positive and productive work culture.

Practical exercise: Look for opportunities to be of service in your interactions with others. Practice active listening, empathy, and kindness. Seek ways to support and uplift those around you, whether it’s through a simple act of kindness, offering a helping hand, or providing guidance and mentorship.

Lever 6: The Lever of Responsibility

The sixth lever Covey explores is the Lever of Responsibility. Responsibility refers to taking ownership of our choices, actions, and outcomes. Covey emphasizes that true success and personal growth can only be achieved when we accept responsibility for our lives and recognize that we have the power to shape our future.

Taking responsibility involves refraining from blaming others or external circumstances for our challenges or failures. It requires a proactive mindset, where we focus on what we can control and take the necessary steps to create the results we desire. Covey encourages individuals to develop a sense of personal accountability and embrace the opportunities for growth that come with taking responsibility.

Example: Imagine you encounter a setback in your career. Instead of blaming your colleagues, the organization, or external factors, you take responsibility for your part in the situation. You reflect on what you could have done differently, learn from the experience, and make a plan to improve your skills or approach. By taking responsibility, you regain control and open doors for personal and professional growth.

Practical exercise: Reflect on areas of your life where you may have been avoiding or deflecting responsibility. Identify specific actions or choices that you can take ownership of. Develop a mindset of personal accountability and commit to making proactive decisions that align with your goals and values.

Lever 7: The Lever of Loyalty

The seventh lever Covey discusses is the Lever of Loyalty. Loyalty involves a steadfast commitment to people, principles, and relationships. Covey emphasizes that loyalty is a two-way street, where we are loyal to others and others reciprocate that loyalty, creating a foundation of trust and support.

Loyalty is expressed through acts of faithfulness, reliability, and dedication. It involves standing by others in times of difficulty, supporting their growth and well-being, and being true to our commitments. Covey highlights the importance of building loyal relationships, both personally and professionally, as they provide a sense of security, collaboration, and shared success.

Example: Consider a loyal friend who consistently shows up for their loved ones during challenging times. They provide emotional support, lend a listening ear, and offer assistance when needed. Their loyalty strengthens the bonds of trust and deepens their relationships, creating a supportive network of individuals who can rely on one another.

Practical exercise: Reflect on the relationships in your life and consider how you can demonstrate loyalty to others. Identify specific actions or gestures that can strengthen your commitment to those you value. Practice being reliable, trustworthy, and supportive, fostering an environment of loyalty in your personal and professional interactions.

Lever 8: The Lever of Reciprocity

The eighth lever Covey explores is the Lever of Reciprocity. Reciprocity involves creating mutually beneficial relationships and fostering a spirit of give and take. Covey emphasizes that by practicing reciprocity, we cultivate a sense of abundance and create opportunities for collaboration and shared success.

Reciprocity goes beyond mere transactions; it involves a genuine desire to contribute and support others without expecting immediate returns. It requires a mindset of abundance, where we believe that there is enough to go around and that helping others ultimately benefits everyone involved. Covey encourages individuals to seek opportunities for reciprocity and embrace a collaborative approach to life.

Example: Imagine you have a colleague who consistently goes above and beyond to support your projects and ideas. In turn, you actively seek ways to reciprocate their support by offering your assistance, providing feedback, or promoting their work. This reciprocal relationship creates a positive cycle of mutual benefit and elevates both individuals to higher levels of success.

Practical exercise: Look for opportunities to practice reciprocity in your relationships and interactions. Offer your support, guidance, or resources to others without expecting immediate returns. Cultivate a mindset of abundance, believing that helping others ultimately benefits the collective and opens doors for collaboration and shared success.

Lever 9: The Lever of Diversity

The ninth lever Covey discusses is the Lever of Diversity. Diversity involves recognizing and appreciating the unique strengths, perspectives, and backgrounds of individuals. Covey emphasizes that embracing diversity leads to innovation, creativity, and a richer understanding of the world.

Diversity is not limited to factors such as race, gender, or ethnicity; it encompasses a broad range of differences, including experiences, skills, and perspectives. Covey encourages individuals to actively seek out diverse perspectives, engage in inclusive conversations, and create environments where everyone feels valued and included. By embracing diversity, we can tap into the collective wisdom and talents of a wide range of individuals.

Example: Consider a team that consists of members from different cultural backgrounds with varied skills and experiences. By fostering an inclusive environment that values and respects each person’s unique contributions, the team benefits from diverse perspectives, creative problem-solving, and innovative ideas. The team’s diversity becomes a strength that propels them towards success.

Practical exercise: Reflect on the diversity within your social and professional circles. Seek out opportunities to engage with individuals from different backgrounds and perspectives. Embrace inclusive conversations and actively listen to different viewpoints. Challenge any biases or preconceptions you may have and foster an environment that celebrates diversity and encourages collaboration.

Lever 10: The Lever of Learning

The tenth lever Covey explores is the Lever of Learning. Learning involves a commitment to continuous growth, development, and expanding our knowledge and skills. Covey emphasizes that lifelong learning is essential for personal and professional success in an ever-evolving world.

Learning requires a mindset of curiosity and a willingness to step outside of our comfort zones. It involves seeking new experiences, acquiring knowledge, and honing our abilities. Covey encourages individuals to embrace a growth mindset, where challenges and setbacks are seen as opportunities for learning and improvement.

Example: Imagine you encounter a new technology or concept in your field of work. Instead of being intimidated or resistant, you approach it with a learning mindset. You invest time and effort in understanding and mastering the new skill or technology. As a result, you expand your capabilities, stay relevant in your industry, and open doors for new opportunities.

Practical exercise: Identify areas of personal or professional growth that you would like to pursue. Create a learning plan that includes reading books, attending seminars or workshops, taking online courses, or seeking mentorship. Embrace a mindset of continuous learning and commit to regularly investing in your knowledge and skill development.

Lever 11: The Lever of Renewal

The eleventh lever Covey discusses is the Lever of Renewal. Renewal involves taking care of our physical, mental, and emotional well-being. Covey emphasizes that self-care and rejuvenation are essential for sustaining high levels of performance and achieving long-term success.

Renewal includes activities such as exercise, proper nutrition, quality sleep, relaxation, reflection, and engaging in hobbies and interests. Covey highlights the importance of creating a balance between work and personal life, setting boundaries, and prioritizing self-care. By investing in renewal, individuals can replenish their energy, reduce stress, and enhance their overall well-being.

Example: Consider an entrepreneur who recognizes the importance of renewal for maintaining peak performance. They prioritize regular exercise, engage in mindfulness practices, and dedicate time to hobbies and activities that bring them joy. By incorporating renewal into their routine, they experience increased focus, creativity, and a higher level of productivity in their work.

Practical exercise: Assess your current self-care practices and identify areas where you can improve. Develop a self-care routine that includes activities that nourish your body, mind, and soul. Prioritize rest, relaxation, and activities that bring you joy. Set boundaries to protect your well-being and ensure a sustainable balance between work and personal life.

Lever 12: The Lever of Teaching

The twelfth and final lever Covey explores is the Lever of Teaching. Teaching involves sharing knowledge, insights, and experiences with others to help them grow and succeed. Covey emphasizes that teaching is a powerful way to reinforce our own learning, make a positive impact, and leave a lasting legacy.

Teaching can take various forms, including mentoring, coaching, leading workshops, or writing. It involves imparting wisdom, offering guidance, and empowering others to reach their full potential. Covey encourages individuals to embrace opportunities to teach and share their knowledge with others, as it not only benefits the learners but also deepens their own understanding and mastery.

Example: Imagine you have acquired expertise in a particular subject or skill. By actively seeking opportunities to teach and share your knowledge, you empower others to learn and grow. Through teaching, you solidify your own understanding, reinforce your expertise, and create a positive impact in the lives of those you mentor or educate.

Practical exercise: Identify areas where you have valuable knowledge or skills to share with others. Look for opportunities to teach, mentor, or coach individuals who can benefit from your expertise. Embrace a mindset of generosity and be open to sharing your knowledge to empower others on their journey of growth and success.

Who is this Book Recommended For?

“Primary Greatness: The 12 Levers of Success” is recommended for individuals who are seeking a holistic approach to personal and professional growth. The book is valuable for anyone who wants to cultivate a meaningful and successful life by aligning their actions with fundamental principles and values. It is suitable for:

  1. Individuals who want to develop their character and become more authentic and principled in their actions.
  2. Those who aspire to make a positive impact on others and contribute to the betterment of society.
  3. Professionals who want to prioritize their goals, manage their time effectively, and achieve work-life balance.
  4. Leaders who aim to build strong, loyal relationships, foster a culture of collaboration, and inspire others to reach their full potential.
  5. Anyone who seeks personal growth, continuous learning, and self-improvement.

Review of the Book

“Primary Greatness: The 12 Levers of Success” is a compelling guide that explores the fundamental principles and values that lead to personal and professional success. Stephen Covey, known for his expertise in self-help and personal development, delivers a thought-provoking and inspiring book that offers practical wisdom for individuals seeking a more meaningful and purpose-driven life.

Covey goes beyond conventional definitions of success and delves into deeper aspects such as integrity, contribution, and service. By addressing these core principles, Covey encourages readers to cultivate their character and make a positive impact on others, creating a ripple effect that extends far beyond personal achievements.

The book is well structured, with each lever explored in detail and accompanied by relatable examples. Covey’s writing style is engaging and accessible, making complex concepts easy to understand and apply in real-life situations. The book is filled with inspiring stories and anecdotes that resonate with readers and reinforce the importance of living according to these principles.

While the book offers valuable insights and guidance, it could benefit from more diversity in perspectives and examples. While Covey covers a broad range of topics, incorporating more diverse experiences and backgrounds would enhance its relevance and resonance with a wider audience.

What we liked:

  • Covey’s emphasis on aligning actions with fundamental principles and values.
  • Practical insights that encourage self-reflection and application of the principles.
  • Engaging storytelling that illustrates the concepts and principles in action.

What needs improvement:

  • Greater diversity in perspectives and examples to make the book more relatable to a wider audience.

Conclusion

“Primary Greatness: The 12 Levers of Success” is a powerful book that offers a comprehensive framework for personal and professional success. Stephen Covey provides readers with twelve foundational principles, or levers, that can unlock their primary greatness and lead to a more meaningful and fulfilling life.

By exploring concepts such as integrity, contribution, responsibility, and renewal, Covey guides readers towards a deeper understanding of what it means to live a successful life. The book offers practical exercises, relatable examples, and engaging storytelling that make the principles accessible and applicable in daily life.

Recommended for individuals seeking personal growth, success, and a greater sense of purpose, “Primary Greatness” serves as a valuable resource that inspires readers to align their actions with their values, make a positive impact on others, and live a life of significance.
