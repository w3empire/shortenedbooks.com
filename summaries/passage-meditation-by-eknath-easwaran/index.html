Passage Meditation by Eknath Easwaran (Summary & Review)

author David Barrett
Rating

“Passage Meditation” is a profound and practical guidebook written by Eknath Easwaran, a renowned spiritual teacher, author, and translator of Indian classics. This book presents a simple yet transformative meditation method that allows individuals to bring the deep wisdom of the heart into their daily lives.

Easwaran’s intention is to provide readers with a practical tool for personal growth and self-discovery, enabling them to cultivate inner peace, clarity, and spiritual fulfillment.

The main themes of the book revolve around meditation, mindfulness, and the power of focused attention. Easwaran emphasizes the practice of “passage meditation,” which involves selecting a spiritual text or mantra and using it as a point of focus during meditation. By repeatedly reciting and reflecting on these passages, individuals can quiet the mind, overcome distractions, and access the inherent wisdom within.

Easwaran encourages readers to integrate meditation into their daily routines, making it a way of life rather than a mere isolated practice. He emphasizes the transformative power of consistent meditation and its ability to bring about positive changes in one’s thoughts, emotions, and actions.

Through the practice of passage meditation, individuals can develop inner resilience, deepen their understanding of themselves and others, and experience a profound connection with the divine.

Idea 1: Cultivating Concentration and Stillness

Easwaran highlights the importance of cultivating concentration and stillness as foundational skills in meditation. He emphasizes that the ability to focus the mind is essential for deepening one’s meditation practice and accessing the wisdom within.

Easwaran suggests starting with short periods of meditation and gradually increasing the duration as your concentration improves. By consistently practicing meditation, you can train your mind to be calm, focused, and receptive.

Practical exercise:

  1. Find a quiet and comfortable place where you won’t be disturbed.
  2. Select a short passage or mantra that resonates with you.
  3. Close your eyes, sit in an upright posture, and begin repeating the passage silently in your mind.
  4. Whenever you notice your attention wandering, gently bring it back to the passage without judgment or frustration.
  5. Start with five minutes of meditation and gradually increase the duration over time.

Idea 2: Transforming Thoughts and Emotions

Easwaran emphasizes that passage meditation is not about suppressing or controlling thoughts and emotions but rather transforming them through a process of deep reflection. By engaging with spiritual passages and mantras, you can gradually cultivate a new way of thinking and feeling, aligned with your highest ideals and aspirations. Easwaran encourages readers to choose passages that resonate with their personal values and goals, allowing them to inspire positive change in their thoughts and emotions.

Example: If you struggle with anger, you can select a passage on forgiveness or compassion as your meditation focus. By repeatedly reflecting on these passages, you can gradually shift your mindset and develop greater patience and understanding in challenging situations.

Practical exercise: Choose a passage that reflects an aspect of yourself or your life that you would like to transform. During meditation, silently recite the passage and reflect on its deeper meaning. Notice how your thoughts and emotions respond to the passage. Over time, observe how your perspective and reactions begin to shift as you internalize the wisdom contained within the passage.

Idea 3: Integration into Daily Life

Easwaran emphasizes the importance of integrating meditation into daily life, extending its benefits beyond the formal practice sessions. He encourages readers to bring mindfulness and focused attention to their everyday activities, such as eating, walking, and interacting with others. By practicing meditation throughout the day, individuals can cultivate a heightened state of awareness and deepen their connection with the present moment.

Practical exercise:

  1. Choose a daily activity that you frequently engage in, such as brushing your teeth or washing dishes.
  2. Before beginning the activity, take a moment to center yourself and bring your attention fully to the task at hand.
  3. Notice the sensations, sounds, and movements involved in the activity.
  4. Whenever you notice your mind wandering, gently bring it back to the present moment.
  5. This practice can help you develop mindfulness and presence in your daily life.

Idea 4: Cultivating Loving Kindness

Easwaran emphasizes the cultivation of loving-kindness, both towards oneself and others, as an essential aspect of passage meditation. He encourages readers to choose passages that inspire feelings of love, compassion, and interconnectedness. By regularly meditating on these passages, individuals can develop a genuine sense of love and goodwill towards themselves and extend it to all beings.

Example: You can choose a passage that affirms the inherent worth and dignity of all individuals. By repeatedly reciting and reflecting on this passage, you can cultivate a deeper sense of respect, empathy, and kindness towards others, regardless of their background or circumstances.

Practical exercise:

  1. Select a passage or mantra that evokes feelings of love and compassion.
  2. During meditation, repeat the passage silently while focusing on the emotions it generates within you.
  3. As you deepen your practice, extend these feelings of loving-kindness to yourself, loved ones, and eventually all beings.
  4. This practice can help you develop a heart-centered approach to life, characterized by empathy, forgiveness, and unconditional love.

Idea 5: Overcoming Negative Habits

Easwaran addresses the challenge of negative habits and offers guidance on how to overcome them through passage meditation. He emphasizes the power of regular practice for reprogramming the mind and replacing unhealthy habits with positive ones. By selecting passages that inspire qualities opposite to the negative habits you wish to overcome, you can gradually transform your thoughts, emotions, and behaviors.

Practical exercise: Identify a negative habit that you would like to overcome. Choose a passage or mantra that reflects the positive quality or behavior you wish to cultivate in its place. During meditation, focus on the passage and internalize its message. As you continue the practice, observe how your thoughts and actions begin to align with the desired positive habit.

Idea 6: Deepening Spiritual Connection

Easwaran emphasizes the importance of deepening one’s spiritual connection through passage meditation. He encourages readers to select passages that resonate with their spiritual beliefs and aspirations, allowing them to establish a direct and personal connection with the divine. By consistently engaging with these passages, individuals can experience a sense of guidance, inspiration, and interconnectedness.

Practical exercise: Choose a passage or mantra that aligns with your spiritual beliefs or aspirations. During meditation, recite the passage and allow its words to deepen your connection with the divine. Reflect on the meaning and significance of the passage in relation to your spiritual journey. Notice how your perception of the divine and your own spirituality evolve over time.

Idea 7: Cultivating Inner Transformation

Easwaran highlights the transformative potential of passage meditation for fostering inner growth and self-discovery. By engaging with passages that address fundamental aspects of human nature, such as love, forgiveness, and compassion, individuals can cultivate these qualities within themselves.

The practice of passage meditation provides a space for self-reflection and introspection, enabling individuals to explore their inner landscape and unlock their true potential.

Practical exercise: Select a passage that resonates with an aspect of yourself that you would like to cultivate or transform. During meditation, immerse yourself in the passage and reflect on its relevance to your personal journey. Notice any shifts in your thoughts, emotions, or perspectives as you continue to engage with the passage. Allow the wisdom within the passage to guide and inspire your inner transformation.

Idea 8: Creating a Sacred Space

Easwaran suggests creating a dedicated and sacred space for meditation as a means to enhance the practice and create a supportive environment. This space can be a physical area in your home or any place that allows you to feel calm, focused, and connected. By designating a specific space for meditation, you create a sense of sacredness and make it easier to establish a consistent practice.

Practical exercise: Find a quiet corner in your home or another location that feels conducive to meditation. Set up a comfortable cushion or chair, and decorate the space with items that hold personal significance or represent your spiritual journey. This space will serve as a reminder and invitation to engage in regular meditation. Make it a habit to return to this sacred space for your practice.

Idea 9: Engaging in Spiritual Fellowship

Easwaran highlights the importance of engaging in spiritual fellowship and connecting with like-minded individuals on the meditation path. Sharing experiences, insights, and challenges with others can provide support, inspiration, and a sense of community. Easwaran suggests seeking out meditation groups or communities where you can participate in group meditations, discussions, and learning opportunities.

Practical exercise: Research and find local meditation groups or communities in your area. Attend group meditation sessions or join online forums and discussion groups centered around passage meditation. Engage in conversations, ask questions, and share your own experiences. Cultivate connections with individuals who share a similar spiritual journey, as their support and insights can enrich your own practice.

Idea 10: Embracing Simplicity and Silence

Easwaran emphasizes the importance of embracing simplicity and silence as a way to create space for inner stillness and deep reflection. In a world filled with noise, distractions, and constant stimulation, intentionally seeking moments of silence and simplicity allows for a deeper connection with oneself and the practice of passage meditation.

Practical exercise:

  1. Set aside specific times during your day for silence and simplicity.
  2. It can be as simple as taking a few minutes each day to sit in silence, free from distractions.
  3. Create intentional pauses throughout your day to disconnect from technology, noise, and external stimuli.
  4. Embrace the simplicity of nature or engage in activities that promote quiet reflection, such as walking in nature or journaling.
  5. Allow these moments of silence and simplicity to deepen your meditation practice and cultivate inner stillness.

Who is this Book Recommended For?

“Passage Meditation: Bringing the Deep Wisdom of the Heart into Daily Life” is recommended for anyone seeking a practical and transformative approach to meditation. The book is suitable for both beginners and experienced practitioners who wish to deepen their meditation practice and integrate it into their daily lives.

Individuals interested in exploring the intersection of spirituality and personal growth will find this book valuable. The teachings of Eknath Easwaran are applicable to people of all religious backgrounds or those with no specific religious affiliation, as passage meditation can be practiced within the framework of one’s own beliefs.

Review of the Book and Conclusion

“Passage Meditation” offers a comprehensive and accessible guide to meditation, presenting a practical method that can be easily incorporated into daily life. Easwaran’s teachings are grounded in his own experiences and conveyed with clarity, depth, and authenticity.

The book’s emphasis on cultivating concentration, transforming thoughts and emotions, integrating meditation into daily life, and cultivating loving kindness provides readers with valuable tools for personal growth and spiritual development.

The method of passage Meditation can be easily understood and practiced by individuals from all walks of life without requiring any specific religious or philosophical beliefs. Easwaran’s guidance is clear and supportive, providing readers with the necessary tools to establish and sustain a meaningful meditation practice.

But while the book provides a solid foundation for passage meditation, some individuals may benefit from additional guidance and resources to address specific challenges or explore different approaches.

In conclusion, “Passage Meditation: Bringing the Deep Wisdom of the Heart into Daily Life” is an essential resource for anyone seeking to incorporate meditation into their daily routine and cultivate inner peace, clarity, and spiritual growth.

Easwaran’s teachings provide a roadmap for personal transformation and offer practical tools to navigate the complexities of modern life. By engaging with the wisdom contained within spiritual passages, readers can embark on a journey of self-discovery and bring greater depth and meaning to their lives.
