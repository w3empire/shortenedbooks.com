Crushing It! by Gary Vaynerchuk (Summary & Review)

author Dean Stewart
Rating

“Crushing It!: How Great Entrepreneurs Build Their Business and Influence-and How You Can, Too” is a bestselling self-help book written by entrepreneur and social media expert Gary Vaynerchuk. The book was published in 2018 and provides a blueprint for individuals looking to build their personal brand and achieve success in the digital age.

The book is a follow-up to Vaynerchuk’s previous book, “Crush It!”, which was published in 2009. In “Crushing It!”, Vaynerchuk shares success stories of people who have followed his advice and leveraged social media to build their personal brand and achieve their goals. The book also includes practical tips and strategies for building a strong personal brand, developing a social media presence, and creating valuable content.

“Crushing It!” has received positive reviews for its practical advice and inspiring success stories. But some critics have noted that the book is geared towards entrepreneurs and may not be as relevant for those in other fields. Nonetheless, it remains a popular and influential book in the world of entrepreneurship and personal branding.

Idea 1: Personal branding is key to success in the digital age

Let’s start by defining what personal branding is. Personal branding is the process of creating a unique image and reputation for oneself based on one’s skills, expertise, and personality. In today’s digital age, personal branding has become more important than ever. With the rise of social media and online platforms, individuals now have more opportunities to showcase their talents and build a following.

But why is personal branding so important? Simply put, it can make or break your success in the digital world. A strong personal brand can help you stand out in a crowded market, attract new clients and customers, and establish your credibility and expertise. On the other hand, a weak or nonexistent personal brand can leave you lost in a sea of competitors, struggling to gain traction and recognition.

So how can you build a strong personal brand that people can trust and relate to? Here are a few tips:

  • Be authentic and genuine: Your personal brand should reflect who you truly are, not who you think you should be. Be honest, transparent, and genuine in your interactions with your audience.
  • Find your niche: Identify your unique skills and strengths, and focus on building your brand around them. This will help you stand out and differentiate yourself from others in your industry.
  • Create valuable content: To build trust and credibility, you need to provide value to your audience. Create content that educates, entertains, or inspires them.
  • Engage with your audience: personal branding is all about building relationships. Engage with your audience by responding to their comments and messages and showing them that you care about their opinions and feedback.
  • Be consistent: Building a strong personal brand takes time and effort. Be consistent in your messaging, tone, and content, and show up regularly to build trust and familiarity with your audience.

To conclude, I would say that Gary Vaynerchuk’s idea that personal branding is key to success in the digital age is spot on. When you build a strong personal brand that people can trust and relate to, you can establish your credibility and expertise, attract new clients and customers, and stand out in a crowded market.

Idea 2: The most successful entrepreneurs are those who are passionate about what they do

This idea encapsulates a vital aspect of entrepreneurship: the role of passion in achieving success. Here, Vaynerchuk emphasizes the importance of being passionate about one’s work and how it can lead to great achievements in business.

Passion is a powerful emotion that can drive individuals to push their limits and achieve their goals. When someone is passionate about what they do, they are more likely to put in the extra effort and dedication needed to excel in their field. This drive can be the difference between success and failure, as passion fuels an entrepreneur’s willingness to put in the time and effort needed to create something great.

Vaynerchuk’s book provides numerous examples of successful entrepreneurs who turned their passion into a thriving business. He explains how individuals who are passionate about their hobbies or interests can leverage their knowledge and skills to build a successful brand. The key is to find a way to monetize that passion and create a business model that works.

Passion also plays a crucial role in inspiring others to follow an entrepreneur’s lead. When someone is truly passionate about what they do, it is contagious. Others are drawn to that passion and energy and want to be a part of it. This is why many successful businesses are built on a strong community of followers who share a common interest or passion.

But take into consideration that passion alone is not enough to guarantee success in entrepreneurship. It must be coupled with a strong work ethic, a willingness to take risks, and a solid business strategy. Passion can drive an entrepreneur to work harder and smarter, but it cannot replace the necessary skills and knowledge needed to run a successful business.

Idea 3: Social media is the best tool for building a personal brand

Your personal brand is the image or reputation that you create for yourself, which distinguishes you from others and helps you achieve your goals. It’s the way you present yourself to the world, both online and offline.

And how does social media come into play? Social media platforms such as Instagram, Twitter, Facebook, and LinkedIn are essentially virtual marketplaces where people share their thoughts, ideas, and experiences. They provide an excellent opportunity to connect with people and showcase your skills, knowledge, and expertise.

According to Vaynerchuk, social media allows you to reach a wide audience, far beyond your immediate network. It’s an excellent platform to showcase your work and demonstrate your value to potential clients, customers, and employers. By creating valuable content and engaging with your followers, you can build a loyal fan base that will support you throughout your career.

But, of course, building a personal brand isn’t easy. It takes time, effort, and consistency. Vaynerchuk advises that you should create content that is authentic, useful, and relevant to your target audience. You should also engage with your followers, respond to comments and messages, and build relationships with people in your industry.

Now you may be asking yourself: What are the benefits of building a personal brand on social media? Well, for starters, it can help you stand out in a crowded job market. If you’re looking for a job, having a strong personal brand can make you more visible to potential employers and give you a competitive edge. If you’re an entrepreneur, a strong personal brand can help you attract customers and investors and grow your business.

To sum things up, social media provides an excellent opportunity to showcase your skills, connect with people, and build a community of loyal supporters. But, as with anything in life, it takes effort, consistency, and patience.

Idea 4: You need to create content that resonates with your audience

So, what exactly does Vaynerchuk mean by “creating content that resonates with your audience”? Well, to put it simply, it means creating content that your audience finds valuable, entertaining, and shareable. But what does that really entail? Let’s break it down.

For one, your content should be valuable. This means that it should offer something of value to your audience. It could be information that they didn’t previously know, a solution to a problem they’re facing, or even just a different perspective on a topic. The key is to ensure that your content provides some sort of value to your audience and is not just self-promotion or fluff.

But not just that; your content should also be entertaining. Let’s face it, we all love to be entertained, and your audience is no different. This doesn’t mean that you have to be a comedian or a master storyteller, but it does mean that you should aim to engage your audience and make your content enjoyable to consume. This could be through the use of humor, anecdotes, or even just a conversational tone.

And lastly, your content should be shareable. In today’s digital age, social media has made it easier than ever for content to be shared across various platforms. Creating shareable content means that your audience will be more likely to spread the word about your brand and help you reach a wider audience.

To create shareable content, it’s important to keep your audience in mind and understand what they would want to share with their own networks. This could be something informative, inspirational, or even just something that’s visually appealing.

Idea 5: Authenticity is crucial

Authenticity is indeed crucial, because Gary Vaynerchuk implores us to embrace our authentic selves in order to succeed in the ever-evolving world of social media. He notes that people are tired of being bombarded with fake, filtered content and are now craving genuine, raw experiences.

In other words, if you want to connect with your audience, you need to let them see the real you.

Authenticity is way more than just posting unedited photos or using raw language. It’s about being true to your values, your beliefs, and your personality. It’s about showing the world who you are, flaws and all.

In the age of influencers and perfectly curated feeds, it can be tempting to present a polished version of ourselves. But according to Vaynerchuk, this is a mistake. Not only is it exhausting to maintain a persona that isn’t true to who we are, but it’s also disingenuous. People can sense when someone is being fake, and it’s a huge turn-off.

Instead, Vaynerchuk urges us to be vulnerable and open. Share your struggles, your failures, and your successes. Let people see the process behind your achievements. This not only makes you more relatable, but it also helps to build trust with your audience.

But keep in mind that there are limits to this. It’s essential to maintain a level of professionalism and privacy, especially if you’re using social media for business purposes. But within those boundaries, don’t be afraid to show your true colors. Be yourself and let your personality shine through.

To put it in fewer words, authenticity means being true to yourself and sharing your genuine self with others. It’s about being open and vulnerable, showing both your strengths and weaknesses. While social media may be a platform for presenting an idealized version of ourselves, it’s important to remember that authenticity is what truly connects with others.

Idea 6: You need to be patient and persistent

So, here, the author highlights the importance of patience and persistence when it comes to creating a successful personal brand. But what does he mean by this, and how can we apply it to our own lives?

To understand Vaynerchuk’s advice, let’s first define again what a personal brand is. A personal brand is the unique combination of skills, experience, and personality that makes you stand out from others in your field. It’s your reputation, your online presence, and your overall image.

Building a personal brand takes time, effort, and dedication, and Vaynerchuk’s message is clear: if you want to succeed, you need to be willing to put in the work.

Now, let’s address another important question: What does it mean to be patient and persistent when building a personal brand? Let’s start with patience. In today’s world of instant gratification, it’s easy to get discouraged when we don’t see immediate results. But building a personal brand is a long-term process, and success doesn’t happen overnight. It takes time to build a following, establish credibility, and earn trust.

Patience means understanding that progress will come, but it may not come as quickly as we would like.

And how about persistence? Well, it means staying committed to your goals even when things get tough. It means not giving up in the face of obstacles or setbacks. Building a personal brand is hard work, and there will be times when it feels like you’re not making any progress. But if you stay persistent, keep pushing forward, and learn from your mistakes, you will eventually see the results you’re looking for.

So how do we apply Vaynerchuk’s advice to our own lives? First, we need to be willing to put in the time and effort it takes to build a personal brand. This means consistently creating valuable content, engaging with our audience, and staying up-to-date with the latest trends in our industry.

We also need to be patient and understand that success will come, but it may not come as quickly as we would like.

Second, we need to stay persistent even when things get tough. This means not giving up when we face obstacles or setbacks, but instead learning from our mistakes and using them as opportunities to grow and improve. We also need to stay focused on our goals and not get distracted by shiny objects or short-term gains.

Idea 7: Collaboration is key to success

To understand this idea, we must first understand the concept of influencers. An influencer is a person who has a large following on social media and can influence the purchasing decisions of their followers.

Influencers come in many forms, from celebrities to bloggers, and each has a unique niche or area of expertise. By partnering with influencers in your niche, you can leverage their audience and reach new customers who may not have heard of your brand otherwise.

But why stop at just one influencer? Collaboration means partnering with multiple influencers to create a network that amplifies your brand’s message. By working together, influencers can create a snowball effect that builds momentum and attracts more followers to the network. This can lead to increased brand awareness, more traffic to your website, and ultimately, more sales.

Collaboration also allows businesses to tap into the expertise and skills of other influencers. Each influencer has their own unique strengths and weaknesses, and by working together, you can create content and campaigns that play to each other’s strengths. For example, if one influencer is great at creating videos and another is an expert in written content, they can collaborate to create a multimedia campaign that reaches a wider audience.

Another benefit of collaboration is that it creates a sense of community among influencers and their followers. By working together, influencers can show that they are part of a larger movement or cause, which can be a powerful motivator for followers to join in and support the cause. This can lead to increased loyalty and engagement among followers, which can translate into more sales and brand advocacy.

However, collaboration is not without its challenges. It requires a high degree of trust and communication among all parties involved. Each influencer has their own brand image and reputation to protect, so it’s important to ensure that everyone is aligned on the goals and values of the collaboration.

Adding to that, there may be conflicts in terms of creative direction or compensation, so it’s important to have clear agreements and expectations from the outset.

Idea 8: You need to be adaptable and flexible

In this part of the book, Vaynerchuk advocates for the importance of adaptability and flexibility in the ever-changing digital landscape. He believes that one must be willing to pivot and adjust their strategies if they want to succeed in today’s market.

Let’s break down the concepts of adaptability and flexibility.

Adaptability means being able to adjust to new situations and challenges. In the digital world, this could mean staying up-to-date with the latest trends and technologies. For instance, when social media platforms like Facebook, Instagram, and Twitter emerged, businesses had to adapt and learn how to use these platforms to their advantage. Those who were slow to adapt were left behind, while those who were quick to pivot their marketing strategies saw great success.

Now, flexibility means being able to change your plans when necessary. This could mean pivoting your business model or adjusting your marketing strategy. Vaynerchuk notes that even the most successful businesses have had to make major changes over the years.

Take Amazon, for example. Originally an online bookstore, Amazon has since expanded to offer a wide variety of products and services. This kind of flexibility has been essential to Amazon’s success, and it’s something that Vaynerchuk believes all entrepreneurs should strive for.

So why are adaptability and flexibility so important? Well, as Vaynerchuk notes, the digital landscape is constantly changing. New technologies and platforms are emerging all the time, and consumer behavior is constantly evolving. In order to stay ahead of the curve, businesses must be willing to adapt and adjust their strategies as needed.

But Vaynerchuk isn’t just talking about adapting to changes in technology and consumer behavior. He’s also talking about adapting to changes in your own life and career. It’s not uncommon for entrepreneurs to start out with one idea or goal, only to discover that their true passion lies elsewhere.

In these cases, Vaynerchuk advises entrepreneurs to be flexible and willing to pivot their careers in a new direction.

Idea 9: Focus on building relationships with your audience

Building relationships with your audience is key to crushing it in today’s digital age. It’s not just about the content you create; it’s about the connection you make.

Now, you may be thinking, “But, I don’t have time to respond to every single comment and message!” And while that may be true, it’s important to remember that building relationships isn’t about quantity; it’s about quality.

So, how do you build quality relationships with your audience? First and foremost, engage with them. Respond to their comments and messages as much as you can. Show them that you’re listening and that you care about what they have to say. After all, social media isn’t a one-way street—it’s a conversation.

But don’t just stop there. Take it a step further and show your audience that you value their input. Ask them questions, run polls, and take their opinions into consideration when creating content. This not only strengthens your relationship with your audience, but it also helps you create content that resonates with them.

Another way to build relationships with your audience is by being authentic, as we discussed before. Don’t be afraid to show your personality and share personal stories. People want to connect with real people, not just a brand or a product. Plus, by being authentic, you’ll attract like-minded individuals who share your values and interests.

And finally, don’t forget to show your appreciation. Whether it’s a simple thank you or a special “shout out,” showing gratitude goes a long way in building strong relationships. Your audience is what makes your brand or business successful, so don’t take them for granted.

Idea 10: Success is not just about making money. It’s about creating a legacy and making a positive impact on the world.

To start, let’s address the first part of Vaynerchuk’s statement: “Success is not just about making money.” Of course, money is important. It allows us to live comfortable lives, provide for our families, and pursue our passions.

But money alone isn’t enough to define success. In fact, many people who are financially successful still feel unfulfilled or unhappy. This is because success is about more than just accumulating wealth. It’s about creating something meaningful that will outlast us.

This brings us to the second part of Vaynerchuk’s statement: “It’s about creating a legacy.” Legacy is a powerful concept that goes beyond our own lifetimes. When we create something that will endure beyond us, we’re contributing to the world in a way that will continue to benefit others for years to come. Whether it’s building a business, creating a work of art, or raising a family, our legacy is the mark we leave on the world.

But creating a legacy isn’t enough on its own. Vaynerchuk emphasizes that success is also about “making a positive impact on the world.” This means that our legacy should be something that makes the world a better place. We should strive to create something that not only endures but also has a positive impact on the people and communities around us.

This could be through philanthropy, advocacy for social justice, or simply creating a product or service that makes people’s lives easier or better.

As a final point, Vaynerchuk’s statement reminds us that success is multi-dimensional. It’s not just about accumulating wealth or achieving personal goals; it’s also about creating something meaningful that will benefit others and make the world a better place.

Who is the book “Crushing It!” by Gary Vaynerchuk recommended for?

The book “Crushing It!” by Gary Vaynerchuk is recommended for entrepreneurs, small business owners, and anyone looking to build a personal brand online. It provides practical advice on how to use social media platforms to grow your business or personal brand, as well as inspirational stories of individuals who have achieved success by following their passions and leveraging the power of social media.

The book is also relevant for anyone interested in the concepts of creating a legacy and making a positive impact on the world, as Vaynerchuk emphasizes these themes throughout the book.

About the author

Gary Vaynerchuk is an entrepreneur, author, speaker, and internet personality. Born in Belarus in 1975, Vaynerchuk immigrated to the United States with his family at the age of three. He grew up in New Jersey and later attended Mount Ida College in Massachusetts, where he started a wine retail business that he grew into a multi-million dollar e-commerce company.

Vaynerchuk is perhaps best known for his work in social media marketing and digital branding. He is the founder and CEO of VaynerMedia, a full-service digital agency that helps businesses build their online presence and engage with their customers on social media platforms. He is also a prolific content creator, producing videos, podcasts, and written content on topics ranging from entrepreneurship and marketing to self-improvement and personal development.

Besides his business ventures, Vaynerchuk is the author of several books, including “Crush It!: Why Now Is the Time to Cash in on Your Passion,” “The Thank You Economy,” and “Jab, Jab, Jab, Right Hook.” He is a popular public speaker and has given keynote addresses at events around the world.

Vaynerchuk has been recognized for his entrepreneurship and digital marketing expertise, receiving numerous awards and accolades, including being named to the Forbes “40 Under 40” list and the Fortune “40 Under 40” list.
