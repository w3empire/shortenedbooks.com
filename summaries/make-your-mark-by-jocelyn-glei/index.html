Make Your Mark by Jocelyn Glei (Summary & Review)

author Jared Wright
Rating

“Make Your Mark: The Creative’s Guide to Building a Business with Impact” by Jocelyn Glei is a comprehensive guidebook that provides valuable insights and practical advice for creative individuals who aspire to build businesses with a meaningful impact.

The book recognizes the unique challenges faced by creatives in the business world and offers strategies to leverage their creative skills while creating a sustainable and purposeful business.

Throughout the book, Jocelyn Glei emphasizes the importance of aligning passion with purpose and explores various aspects of entrepreneurship, such as ideation, branding, marketing, and collaboration.

She combines her own experiences as a writer and creative director with interviews and case studies of successful creative entrepreneurs, providing readers with a rich and diverse range of perspectives and practical examples.

Glei’s intention with this book is to inspire and empower creative individuals to embrace their unique talents and turn their creative passions into successful businesses that make a positive impact on the world.

By offering a roadmap for navigating the challenges of entrepreneurship, she aims to help readers overcome self-doubt, find their unique value proposition, and build businesses that align with their values and aspirations.

Idea 1: Embrace Your Creative Identity

This idea is all about the importance of embracing your creative identity and recognizing the unique strengths and perspectives that creatives bring to the business world. Glei encourages you to embrace your creativity as a valuable asset and to leverage it to differentiate yourself in the marketplace. By acknowledging and celebrating your creative identity, you can tap into your authentic self and infuse your business with a distinct personality and style.

Example: Imagine you are a graphic designer who specializes in hand-drawn illustrations. Instead of trying to conform to the traditional norms of graphic design, you can embrace your unique style and offer clients a fresh and artistic approach to their design needs. By positioning yourself as a creative with a specific identity, you can attract clients who appreciate your artistic sensibilities and are willing to pay a premium for your distinctive work.

Practical exercise: Take some time to reflect on your creative strengths and passions. Identify the unique qualities that set you apart from others in your field. Consider how you can integrate these qualities into your business to create a distinctive brand and attract the right audience.

Idea 2: Define Your Purpose and Values

To build a business with impact, it is crucial to define your purpose and values. Glei emphasizes the importance of understanding the deeper why behind your business and aligning your work with your values. By having a clear sense of purpose, you can make decisions and set goals that are in line with your larger vision, ensuring that your business remains meaningful and fulfilling.

Example: Let’s say you are a fashion designer passionate about sustainable and ethical practices. By defining your purpose as creating environmentally conscious and socially responsible fashion, you can guide your business decisions, from sourcing materials to manufacturing processes. This purpose-driven approach will not only attract like-minded customers but also create a positive impact on the fashion industry as a whole.

Practical exercise: Take some time to reflect on your personal values and the impact you want to make through your business. Write a mission statement that captures your purpose and values, and refer back to it regularly as a guiding principle in your decision-making process.

Idea 3: Cultivate a Growth Mindset

A growth mindset is essential for navigating the challenges and uncertainties of entrepreneurship. Glei emphasizes the importance of embracing a mindset that embraces learning, resilience, and adaptability. Instead of viewing failures as setbacks, a growth mindset sees them as opportunities for growth and improvement. By cultivating this mindset, you can overcome obstacles, embrace change, and continuously evolve your business.

Example: Suppose you launch a new product that doesn’t perform as well as expected. Instead of giving up or dwelling on the failure, you can adopt a growth mindset and view it as a learning experience. Analyze what went wrong, gather feedback from customers, and use that knowledge to iterate and improve your product. This resilience and willingness to learn from setbacks will ultimately contribute to your long-term success.

Practical exercise: Identify an area of your business where you have faced challenges or setbacks. Reflect on what you have learned from those experiences and identify specific actions you can take to apply that learning and improve the situation.

Idea 4: Build a Supportive Network

Building a supportive network is crucial for creative entrepreneurs. Glei emphasizes the value of surrounding yourself with like-minded individuals who understand the unique challenges of the creative journey. By connecting with other creatives and entrepreneurs, you can gain inspiration, receive feedback, and access valuable resources and opportunities.

Example: Joining a mastermind group or attending industry conferences can provide opportunities to meet and connect with fellow creatives. Through these networks, you can collaborate on projects, share insights and advice, and find mentors who can guide you on your entrepreneurial journey. Building relationships within your industry can open doors to new opportunities and help you navigate the challenges of building a business.

Practical exercise: Identify networking opportunities within your industry or creative community. Attend events, join online communities, and actively seek out connections with like-minded individuals. Nurture these relationships by offering support and value to others, and you will find that they will reciprocate.

Idea 5: Master the Art of Storytelling

In “Make Your Mark,” Glei emphasizes the power of storytelling as a tool for building a compelling brand and connecting with your audience. By crafting a narrative around your business, you can evoke emotions, create a sense of authenticity, and differentiate yourself from competitors. Storytelling allows you to communicate your values, mission, and unique selling points in a way that resonates with your target audience.

Example: Consider the success of companies like TOMS Shoes, which has built a brand around the story of giving back. By emphasizing their mission to donate a pair of shoes for every purchase, TOMS Shoes has connected with customers who want to make a positive impact through their purchases. The power of storytelling has allowed TOMS Shoes to create a loyal customer base that aligns with their values.

Practical exercise: Reflect on the story behind your business. Identify the key elements that make your story unique and compelling. Craft a narrative that communicates your values and mission, and integrate this storytelling into your branding and marketing efforts.

Idea 6: Embrace Experimentation and Iteration

In the ever-changing landscape of business, experimentation and iteration are crucial for success. Glei encourages you to embrace a mindset of constant exploration and refinement. By testing new ideas, gathering feedback, and adapting based on the results, you can refine your products, services, and strategies to better meet the needs of your audience.

Example: Consider the approach taken by companies like Spotify. Instead of launching a fully formed product, Spotify released an MVP (Minimum Viable Product) and continuously iterated based on user feedback. This iterative approach allowed them to refine their product, address user needs, and ultimately become one of the leading music streaming platforms.

Practical exercise: Identify an aspect of your business that you would like to improve or innovate. Develop a plan for experimentation, including specific tests or prototypes that you can implement. Gather feedback from your audience or customers, and use that feedback to refine and iterate on your ideas.

Idea 7: Cultivate a Mindful Approach to Time Management

Effective time management is crucial for creative entrepreneurs, who often juggle multiple projects and responsibilities. Glei emphasizes the importance of cultivating a mindful approach to time management, where you prioritize tasks based on their impact and align your time with your larger goals.

By adopting strategies such as time blocking, setting boundaries, and practicing self-care, you can optimize your productivity and maintain a healthy work-life balance.

Example: Implementing the Pomodoro Technique, where you work in focused bursts followed by short breaks, can help you maintain focus and prevent burnout. By setting clear boundaries around your work hours and prioritizing self-care activities, such as exercise and relaxation, you can recharge and ensure that you bring your best self to your creative work.

Practical exercise: Evaluate your current approach to time management. Identify any areas where you struggle to prioritize or maintain focus. Experiment with different time management techniques, such as time blocking or task prioritization, and observe the impact on your productivity and well-being.

Idea 8: Embrace Collaboration and Partnerships

Collaboration and partnerships are powerful tools for creative entrepreneurs. Glei highlights the value of collaborating with others who complement your skills and strengths. By joining forces, you can leverage each other’s expertise, resources, and networks to create innovative projects and reach a wider audience. Collaboration also fosters a sense of community and support, which can alleviate the isolation often experienced by creatives.

Example: Consider the collaboration between Nike and Apple to create the Nike+ app. By combining Nike’s expertise in athletic footwear and apparel with Apple’s technology, they created a product that seamlessly integrates fitness tracking and music streaming. This collaboration expanded the reach of both brands and provided a unique offering to their customers.

Practical exercise: Identify individuals or businesses in your industry or related fields with whom you can collaborate. Reach out to them and explore potential opportunities for joint projects or partnerships. Be open to sharing your expertise and resources, and look for ways to create mutually beneficial relationships.

Idea 9: Embrace Constraints and Foster Creativity

Constraints can be catalysts for creativity. Glei emphasizes the value of embracing limitations and using them as fuel for innovation. Instead of seeing constraints as obstacles, view them as opportunities to think outside the box and find creative solutions. By reframing constraints as challenges to overcome, you can unlock new possibilities and create unique offerings.

Example: The limited character count on Twitter initially seemed like a constraint. However, it forced users to distill their thoughts into concise and impactful messages, leading to the rise of a new form of communication. This constraint fostered creativity and revolutionized the way we share information online.

Practical exercise: Identify a current constraint or limitation in your business or creative process. Instead of seeing it as a hindrance, brainstorm ways to leverage that constraint to your advantage. Explore alternative approaches or solutions that can help you turn the limitation into a unique selling point.

Idea 10: Practice Reflection and Continuous Learning

Reflection and continuous learning are essential for personal and professional growth. Glei encourages you to set aside time for introspection and evaluation. By reflecting on your experiences, successes, and failures, you can gain valuable insights and refine your approach. Additionally, she emphasizes the importance of ongoing learning, whether through books, courses, or mentorship, to stay informed and adapt to the evolving business landscape.

Example: Take the time to reflect on a recent project or initiative. What worked well? What could be improved? By analyzing your experiences and gathering feedback, you can identify areas for growth and refine your strategies for future endeavors.

Practical exercise: Schedule regular time for reflection and evaluation. Create a habit of journaling or engaging in self-assessment to capture your insights and observations. Identify specific areas where you would like to expand your knowledge or skills, and seek out resources or learning opportunities to support your continuous learning journey.

Who is this Book Recommended For?

“Make Your Mark: The Creative’s Guide to Building a Business with Impact” is recommended for creative individuals who aspire to build businesses that align with their values and make a positive impact. It is particularly relevant for:

  • Creatives, such as writers, designers, artists, musicians, and performers, who want to leverage their talents and skills to build successful businesses.
  • Aspiring entrepreneurs who are passionate about making a difference and want to create businesses that align with their values.
  • Individuals who have already started a creative business but are looking for guidance and strategies to enhance their impact and sustainability.
  • Those seeking inspiration and practical advice from successful creative entrepreneurs and industry experts.

This book provides valuable insights and actionable strategies for creative individuals who are navigating the challenges of entrepreneurship and want to build businesses with purpose and impact.

Review of the Book and Conclusion

“Make Your Mark: The Creative’s Guide to Building a Business with Impact” is an excellent resource for creative entrepreneurs who are seeking guidance and inspiration in their journey. Jocelyn Glei offers a comprehensive roadmap for building a business that aligns with personal values and creates a positive impact.

The book is filled with practical advice, real-life examples, and thought-provoking exercises that help readers apply the concepts to their own businesses.

The book excels in its focus on addressing the distinctive challenges encountered by creatives in the business world. Glei understands the intricacies of the creative process and provides tailored strategies that allow creatives to leverage their skills and strengths.

The inclusion of interviews and case studies from successful creative entrepreneurs adds depth and diversity to the book, offering a range of perspectives and insights.

The book’s practical insights are particularly valuable, as they encourage readers to take action and apply the concepts to their own businesses. The exercises prompt reflection, strategic thinking, and experimentation, enabling readers to make tangible progress and implement the ideas presented in the book.

However, one aspect that could be improved is the depth of coverage on certain topics. Some ideas and concepts are introduced but not explored in great detail, leaving readers wanting more in-depth analysis or additional resources for further exploration.

In conclusion, “Make Your Mark: The Creative’s Guide to Building a Business with Impact” is a priceless guide for creative entrepreneurs who want to build businesses that align with their values and make a positive impact.

It offers practical guidance, inspiring examples, and thought-provoking exercises that empower creatives to navigate the challenges of entrepreneurship and create businesses that leave a lasting mark on the world.

Whether you are just starting out or looking to enhance the impact of your existing business, this book provides valuable insights and strategies to help you succeed in your creative journey.
