Living Forward by Michael Hyatt (Summary & Review)

author Mark Wheeler
Rating

“Living Forward: A Proven Plan to Stop Drifting and Get the Life You Want” is a self-help book written by Michael Hyatt and Daniel Harkavy. The authors aim to provide readers with a strategic approach to living a purposeful life by creating a personal life plan.

The book emphasizes the importance of intentional living, guiding individuals to take control of their lives and make decisions aligned with their values and long-term goals.

The main theme of the book revolves around the idea that living without direction leads to a sense of drift and dissatisfaction. The authors present a step-by-step process to help readers design and implement a comprehensive life plan, enabling them to prioritize their time, energy, and resources effectively.

Through practical tools, insights, and real-life examples, Hyatt and Harkavy empower readers to take charge of their lives, identify their life’s purpose, and establish a clear path towards achieving their goals.

Idea 1: Create a Written Life Plan

The authors stress the significance of creating a written life plan as the foundation for intentional living. This involves defining the desired outcomes in various areas of life, such as career, relationships, health, and personal development.

By documenting these aspirations, individuals gain clarity and can better align their actions with their long-term goals. The written life plan serves as a guide to making intentional choices, avoiding distractions, and staying focused on what truly matters.

Practical exercise: Take time to reflect on your life’s vision and goals. Write down your aspirations in different areas of life, ensuring they are specific, measurable, achievable, relevant, and time-bound (SMART). Use these statements as a basis for creating your written life plan.

Idea 2: Identify Your Life Accounts

To live intentionally, the authors introduce the concept of life accounts, which represent the key areas of an individual’s life. These accounts typically include categories like health, family, career, finances, personal growth, and spirituality. By recognizing these accounts, individuals can evaluate their current state and set specific goals to enhance each area. This approach helps individuals achieve balance and ensure that they invest time and energy in all significant aspects of their lives.

Example: If someone realizes they have been neglecting their health due to work commitments, they can allocate specific goals and actions to improve their physical well-being. This may include exercising regularly, adopting a healthy eating plan, and scheduling regular health check-ups.

Idea 3: Determine Your Legacy

One essential aspect of a life plan is determining the legacy an individual wants to leave behind. The authors emphasize the importance of considering the impact and influence individuals desire to have on others. By defining their legacy, individuals can align their daily actions with their long-term goals and values, ensuring they contribute meaningfully to the lives of others.

Example: If someone wants to be remembered as a compassionate leader who empowered others, they can incorporate acts of kindness and mentorship into their daily routine. By consistently displaying these behaviors, they will build a legacy aligned with their values and positively impact the lives of those around them.

Practical exercise: Spend time reflecting on the kind of legacy you want to leave behind. Consider the impact you desire to have on others, the values you want to instill, and the ways you can contribute to the betterment of society. Incorporate these reflections into your life plan.

Idea 4: Conduct a Life Assessment

To gain a clear understanding of one’s current state, the authors suggest conducting a life assessment. This involves evaluating the different life accounts, examining strengths and weaknesses, and identifying areas that require improvement. The life assessment helps individuals identify gaps between their current reality and their desired future, enabling them to develop targeted strategies for personal growth and progress.

Example: During a life assessment, someone may realize that their relationships lack quality time and meaningful connections. This assessment can serve as a wake-up call, prompting them to prioritize building stronger bonds with loved ones and dedicating more time to nurturing those relationships.

Practical exercise: Assess each life account individually. Reflect on your satisfaction level in each area and identify your strengths and weaknesses. Consider the areas that require improvement and brainstorm specific actions you can take to enhance those accounts.

Idea 5: Set Priorities and Create Action Steps

To bridge the gap between current reality and desired outcomes, the authors stress the importance of setting priorities and creating action steps. Prioritizing involves selecting the most significant goals and determining the actions necessary to accomplish them. By breaking down larger goals into smaller, actionable steps, individuals can make progress incrementally and maintain motivation along the way.

Example: Suppose someone’s priority is to advance in their career. They can set specific goals, such as acquiring new skills, networking with industry professionals, and seeking mentorship. These actions enable them to focus their energy and resources on professional development and increase their chances of achieving their career goals.

Practical exercise: Review your life plan and identify the most important goals in each life account. Break these goals down into actionable steps and create a timeline for their completion. Assign specific deadlines and track your progress regularly.

Idea 6: Implement Routines and Habits

To ensure consistent progress towards the goals outlined in the life plan, the authors highlight the importance of implementing effective routines and habits. Routines provide structure and enable individuals to allocate time and energy efficiently. By establishing habits aligned with their priorities, individuals can make progress on their goals without relying solely on willpower and motivation.

Example: If someone’s goal is to improve their physical fitness, they can establish a routine that includes regular exercise and healthy meal preparation. By making these activities habitual, individuals can overcome resistance and effortlessly integrate them into their daily lives.

Practical exercise: Identify the routines and habits that will support the achievement of your goals. Determine specific actions you can incorporate into your daily or weekly routine to ensure consistent progress. Experiment with different strategies and adjust as necessary.

Idea 7: Leverage Accountability

Accountability plays a crucial role in sustaining motivation and ensuring progress. The authors emphasize the power of accountability partners or groups to provide support, encouragement, and feedback throughout the journey. Sharing goals, progress, and challenges with trusted individuals enhances commitment and increases the likelihood of achieving desired outcomes.

Practical exercise: Find an accountability partner or join a mastermind group with like-minded individuals who are also committed to intentional living and personal growth. Regularly connect with your accountability partner or group to share progress, discuss challenges, and offer support to each other.

Idea 8: Practice Self-Care and Renewal

To maintain the energy and enthusiasm necessary for long-term success, the authors emphasize the importance of self-care and renewal. Taking care of physical, mental, and emotional well-being allows individuals to operate at their best and avoid burnout. Incorporating activities that provide relaxation, rejuvenation, and personal fulfillment is essential for maintaining a healthy and balanced life.

Example: Engaging in activities like exercise, meditation, hobbies, spending time in nature, or pursuing creative outlets can contribute to personal renewal. These practices provide individuals with the necessary energy and mental clarity to pursue their goals effectively.

Practical exercise: Identify self-care activities that align with your interests and values. Schedule regular time for these activities in your calendar to ensure you prioritize self-care and renewal.

Idea 9: Embrace Course Corrections

As individuals progress towards their goals, it is essential to embrace the possibility of course corrections. The authors emphasize that life is dynamic and circumstances may change. Being open to adjustments, reevaluating priorities, and making necessary changes to the life plan allows individuals to adapt and stay on track, even when faced with unexpected challenges or new opportunities.

Practical exercise: Regularly review your life plan and assess whether it is still aligned with your evolving values, goals, and circumstances. Identify areas that require adjustments and make the necessary changes to ensure continued alignment with your desired outcomes.

Idea 10: Live with Intention Every Day

Ultimately, the book emphasizes the importance of living with intention every day. Intentional living requires consistently aligning actions with values, making conscious choices, and maintaining a focus on long-term goals. By developing a mindset of intentionality, individuals can create a purposeful and fulfilling life.

Example: Instead of allowing distractions and external circumstances to dictate their day, individuals who live with intention make deliberate choices about how they allocate their time, energy, and resources. They prioritize activities that align with their goals and values, and they proactively pursue personal growth and fulfillment.

Practical exercise: Continually remind yourself of your life plan and the intentions you have set for each day. Regularly reflect on your actions and choices, ensuring they align with your long-term goals and values. Be mindful of your daily decisions and strive to live each day with purpose.

Who is this Book Recommended For?

“Living Forward” is recommended for individuals who feel a sense of drift or dissatisfaction in their lives and desire to regain control and purpose. The book is suitable for anyone seeking guidance on creating a comprehensive life plan and living intentionally. It is particularly valuable for those who are ready to invest time and effort into self-reflection, goal-setting, and personal growth.

This book will resonate with individuals who are seeking a practical framework to align their actions with their values and long-term goals. It provides valuable insights and tools for those who wish to escape the trap of drifting through life and are committed to taking proactive steps towards the life they truly desire.

Review of the Book

“Living Forward” offers a comprehensive and actionable approach to living a purposeful life. The book provides a clear framework for creating a life plan and offers practical tools, exercises, and examples to support readers throughout their journey. The emphasis on intentional living and aligning actions with values resonates strongly, making it a compelling read for those seeking guidance in this area.

What we liked:

  • The step-by-step process for creating a written life plan is well-structured and easy to follow.
  • The inclusion of real-life examples helps readers understand the concepts and see how they can be applied in practice.
  • The emphasis on self-care and renewal as essential components of intentional living is refreshing and important.

What needs improvement:

  • While the book provides guidance on setting priorities and creating action steps, more in-depth strategies for overcoming obstacles and maintaining motivation could be beneficial.
  • Some readers may find the repetitive nature of certain concepts and examples slightly redundant.

“Living Forward” is an essential tool for people seeking to take control of their lives, clarify their goals, and live with intention. It offers practical guidance and a roadmap for designing a life plan that aligns with one’s values and aspirations. By implementing the ideas presented in the book, readers can cultivate a sense of purpose, make intentional choices, and achieve their desired outcomes.

Conclusion

“Living Forward: A Proven Plan to Stop Drifting and Get the Life You Want” by Michael Hyatt and Daniel Harkavy provides a comprehensive framework for intentional living through the creation of a written life plan. By guiding readers through the process of identifying their priorities, setting goals, and aligning their actions with their values, the book empowers individuals to live with purpose and fulfillment.

The ten ideas discussed in this summary highlight the key concepts of the book, ranging from creating a written life plan and identifying life accounts to practicing self-care and embracing course corrections. Through practical exercises and examples, readers are equipped with the tools and insights necessary to implement these ideas in their own lives.

To put it briefly, “Living Forward” offers a compelling and actionable approach to intentional living. By following the principles outlined in the book, readers can navigate the complexities of life with clarity, focus, and a sense of direction. This book serves as a valuable resource for anyone seeking to stop drifting and create the life they truly want.
